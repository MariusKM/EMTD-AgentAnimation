## Role
Light ranged infantry unit for EMTD — a hooded ranger / scout-archer, the rank-and-file bowman of the player's army. He reads as scrappy, alert, and capable rather than heroic; one of many, not a named hero. Tone follows the project-wide Supercell / Kingshot direction: stylized, jovial, slightly comical, expressive even in a small-scale unit silhouette.

## Style Anchor
- Stylized mobile game character in the Supercell / Kingshot lineage.
- Chibi proportions, approximately **2.5–3 heads tall**; oversized head ~30–35% of total height; slightly enlarged hands; chunky wide-soled boots.
- Hand-painted digital illustration look — soft painted shading, visible brushwork, rich saturated colors, warm shadow tones (never gray/cool).
- Single dominant warm top-left key light, soft fill from the opposite side; soft painted highlights on metal, leather, and arrow tips.
- **Thin black outline** on the full character silhouette — a clean, even, slightly tapered line in near-black (not pure 0,0,0; a deep warm brown-black). Consistent line weight all the way around the figure, with a very slight thickening in shadowed recesses (under the chin, beneath the cape, inside the hood opening) and a slight thinning where forms catch the key light. The outline reads as a deliberate hand-drawn ink stroke, not as a glow or a digital stroke effect.
- Interior linework is **much softer / thinner** than the silhouette outline, and is mostly implied through value changes rather than drawn lines. Where interior lines do appear (between the tunic panels, the boot cuff fold, the quiver rim, the belt edge, the cape inner curl, the bandage layer overlaps) they sit one or two values darker than the local color — never a hard black.
- Three-quarter view, full body, clean white background, no environmental context, minimal contact shadow.

## Build & Proportions
- **Overall**: Slim and athletic for the chibi rig — lighter and lankier than the warrior/hero builds, but still compact and stocky in the Kingshot way.
- **Height**: ~2.5–3 heads tall.
- **Head**: Oversized, roughly 30–35% of total figure height, with the hood adding visual mass.
- **Torso**: Short and tapered — narrow waist, modest shoulder width.
- **Arms**: Bare from the shoulder down, slim with a soft hint of tone — clearly a hunter's arms, not a warrior's slabs of muscle. Hands are slightly oversized for gestural readability.
- **Legs**: Short and slightly bowed in the standard chibi way.
- **Feet**: Chunky, wide, planted — typical EMTD grounded silhouette.

## Face
- **Skin**: Warm peachy-tan base with soft orange/pink undertones; faint rosy cheek and nose-tip blush; warm subsurface feel; subtle warm-shadow ambient occlusion under the hood and along the jaw.
- **Hair**: Dark brown / near-black, short and slightly tousled. Mostly hidden under the hood, with a few short strands visible at the temples and a small fringe at the forehead. No visible hair behind the head — the hood swallows it.
- **Eyebrows**: Thick, dark brown, prominent. Slightly angled — the inner ends pulled down a touch and one (his anatomical left, viewer-right) arched a little higher, giving a confident roguish read.
- **Eyes**: Large and expressive in the chibi way; warm brown irises with a clear catchlight highlight in each. Eye shape is moderately narrowed and angled — alert and focused, not wide and innocent. Whites of the eyes visible.
- **Nose**: Small to medium, slightly upturned, with a soft painted shadow on the underside and a faint rosy tint on the tip.
- **Mouth**: Closed confident half-smirk — corners of the lips lifted asymmetrically (the viewer-left corner pulled higher), lips simple and clean, no teeth visible.
- **Jaw / chin**: Soft rounded chin; **clean-shaven** smooth jawline with no stubble shadow (revised 2026-05-05 — the original spec called for a faint stubble shadow, but the model over-rendered it as a five o'clock shadow at L8 once the half-visor framed the lower face. Face anchor is now clean-shaven; do NOT describe stubble in any future prompt body).
- **Ears**: Hidden by the hood.
- **Overall expression read**: Cocky, focused, mildly mischievous — a young hunter who clearly enjoys his job.

## Apparel — Head
- **Hood**: Deep crimson-red pointed hood, fully pulled up over the head. The hood material is matte cloth with soft painted folds and a clear darker inner shadow framing the face. The peak of the hood droops slightly forward over the crown rather than standing rigid. The hood's front opening is a soft oval that frames the face from mid-forehead to chin.
- **Hood interior**: Slightly darker red on the inside fabric, painted in shadow tones; no separate lining color — same red garment family inside and out.
- **Hood seam / trim**: Plain — no visible stitching, no fur trim, no metallic edge. Just a clean rolled fabric edge.
- **No helmet, no headband, no insignia**.

## Apparel — Torso
- **Tunic — silhouette**: Sleeveless, hip-length, fitted at the waist. Cut straight across at the shoulders so the bare arms are fully visible from the deltoid down.
- **Tunic — color blocking**: Mustard-yellow / warm gold base with a vertical **red center panel** running the full length of the chest from collar to hem, and matching **red side panels** along each flank. The result is a clear three-band silhouette — red / yellow / red — when read across the torso.
- **Tunic — material**: Matte hand-painted cloth with subtle vertical fold lines; soft warm highlights along the chest and down the center seam; warm-shadow tones in the side recesses.
- **Tunic — neckline**: Simple round crew-style neckline, plain hem, no collar.
- **Tunic — hem**: Falls just below the belt line at mid-hip; clean cut edge, no fringe or scallop.
- **Belt**: Single wide warm-brown leather belt at the natural waist, slightly worn / scuffed. Painted leather grain texture.
- **Belt buckle**: Small rectangular brass / bronze buckle centered at the front, with a soft buttery-warm metallic highlight (not chrome-yellow).
- **No undershirt visible, no shoulder pauldron, no chest plate, no tabard.**

## Apparel — Cape
- **Cape**: Crimson-red cloak attached at both shoulders (matching the hood — one continuous red garment family), falling behind the body to roughly knee level.
- **Drape**: Hangs naturally with soft painted vertical folds. On the viewer-left side it curls inward slightly, showing a small wedge of the slightly darker inner cape lining; on the viewer-right side it falls straighter behind the bow arm.
- **Length**: Reaches to about mid-shin / knee — not floor-length, not waist-short.
- **Edge**: Plain rolled hem, no fringe, no embroidery, no fur trim.
- **Attachment**: No visible clasp, brooch, or tie at the throat — the cape simply joins the hood/shoulder mass as one red silhouette.

## Apparel — Arms & Hands
- **Arms**: Both biceps are bare from the shoulder down to roughly mid-forearm. Skin tone matches the face — warm peachy-tan with soft painted shading at the deltoid, bicep, and forearm. No tattoos, no armbands, no upper-arm bracers.
- **Anatomical-LEFT forearm + hand** (viewer-right side of image): The forearm is wrapped in a **brown leather archer's wrist bracer** running from wrist to mid-forearm — matte warm-brown leather (same palette as the belt and boots), painted with subtle leather grain and a single visible cross-strap binding, soft warm highlights. The hand itself is bare and grips the bow vertically at hip height — fingers wrapped around the bow's leather-bound grip, thumb resting on the front of the grip.
- **Anatomical-RIGHT forearm + hand** (viewer-left side of image): The forearm and hand are wrapped from knuckles to mid-forearm in a clean **off-white linen / cloth bandage**. Painted with soft fold lines and subtle warm shadow in the wrap recesses; the wrap layers are visible as overlapping bands. The wrapped hand is held forward across the body at chest / waist height with fingers loosely curled — reads as the draw hand, ready to nock an arrow. (The asymmetry — leather bracer on the bow arm, linen bandage on the draw hand — is intentional: the bow arm needs structural protection from bowstring snap, the draw hand only needs to protect the string-side fingers.)
- **Rings, gauntlets, additional bracers, upper-arm armor**: None.

## Apparel — Lower Body
- **Trousers**: Olive-green / muted forest-green cloth pants, knee-length to just below the knee, slightly loose / bloused. Soft painted vertical fold lines; no stripes, no patches, no decorative trim.
- **Color**: A muted desaturated forest green with warm undertones — not bright lime, not military olive-drab.
- **Cuff**: Loose cuff that gathers slightly above the boot.
- **No leg armor, no greaves, no leg wraps below the knee.**

## Apparel — Boots
- **Style**: Chunky warm-brown leather ankle-to-mid-shin boots with a wide flat sole. Standard EMTD chibi-grounded boot silhouette.
- **Cuff**: A folded-down upper cuff at the ankle in slightly darker brown, giving each boot a clear two-tone stack (lighter shaft, darker fold).
- **Sole**: Wide and flat with a slightly darker bottom edge; no visible tread pattern.
- **Laces / buckles**: None visible — clean leather pull-on look.
- **Material**: Painted matte leather with subtle warm highlights on the toe box and the cuff fold.

## Apparel — Quiver
- **Type**: Brown leather back-quiver, cylindrical, slung diagonally across the back.
- **Strap**: Brown leather shoulder strap running diagonally across the chest from the **anatomical-left shoulder** down to the **anatomical-right hip** (so the quiver body rises behind the **viewer-right side of the head**). The strap is partially obscured by the cape but visible where it crosses the shoulder.
- **Body**: Plain rounded leather tube with painted darker top and bottom rims. No metal banding, no decorative tooling, no insignia.
- **Contents**: Three to four arrows visible rising out of the top, fletched ends up. Fletchings are **white feathers with red bindings / red tips at the nock**. Arrow shafts are pale wood-tan, just visible between the fletching and the quiver lip.
- **Arrowheads**: Not visible (pointing down inside the quiver).

## Weapon — Bow
- **Type**: Recurve-style **short bow**. Single curved limb profile with the limb tips bending slightly away from the archer at the ends — clearly a hunter's recurve, not a longbow, not a crossbow, not a compound bow.
- **Material — limbs**: Warm wood-brown, painted with soft grain highlights and a slightly darker inner-curve shadow. Two-tone where the upper and lower limbs taper to the tips.
- **Material — grip**: Darker brown leather wrap around the central handle, with painted cross-binding texture.
- **Bowstring**: Thin pale off-white / cream string running from limb tip to limb tip, drawn as a clean single line with a small painted highlight.
- **Length**: Short — when held vertically at the hip, the bow reaches from roughly the belt line down to just above the knee, and up to roughly mid-chest. Compact, mobile bow appropriate for a light-infantry archer.
- **Held**: Vertically in the **anatomical-left hand** at hip height, gripped at the leather wrap, string facing forward toward the viewer.

## Pose Description
Three-quarter view, body angled roughly 15–25° to the viewer's left so the character's anatomical right side faces camera. Weight planted evenly on both feet with a very slight forward lean.

**Bow arm (anatomical-LEFT, viewer-RIGHT side of frame)**: Hangs DOWN at his side with the elbow only slightly bent. The bow is held VERTICALLY at HIP height directly in front of the hip — the hand grips the bow's central leather grip wrap and sits roughly at the level of the wide leather belt. The bow is NOT raised, NOT pulled across the body, NOT extended forward. The bow's upper limb rises to roughly mid-chest height; the lower limb drops to roughly above-knee height.

**Draw arm (anatomical-RIGHT, viewer-LEFT side of frame)**: Bent sharply at the elbow at roughly **90 degrees**, with the **forearm held HORIZONTALLY across the chest** at upper-chest / sternum level (parallel to the ground). The bandaged hand sits at the **center of the chest** area, slightly toward the bow side, in a **tight closed FIST** shape (fingers curled into the palm, NOT an open hand, NOT loosely splayed, NOT reaching). The fist is held with the **knuckles facing outward** toward the viewer and the back of the hand visible. The fist does NOT touch the bow, the bowstring, or the chest — it hovers at chest center as a static ready-stance fist.

**The pose reads as a calm balanced HERO STANCE**: bow arm down at hip on the viewer-right, bent draw arm horizontal across the chest with closed fist at center on the viewer-left. It is NOT an active draw, NOT a nocking motion, NOT a reach — both arms are static and balanced.

**Quiver, cape, head**: The quiver rises behind the viewer-right side of the head with the white-fletched arrows clearly silhouetted against the hood. The cape falls straight behind the body, trailing softly down to about knee level with a slight inward curl on the viewer-left side. The hood frames the face in soft inner shadow but the eyes and smirk remain clearly readable — the face is the focal point of the silhouette.
