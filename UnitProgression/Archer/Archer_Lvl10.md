> **Status**: One of two ground-truth anchors for the Archer unit (the other is `archer1.png` / Lvl 1). The intermediate `Progression/archer2…archer9.png` set is **reference only** — it shows how a progression *could* arc but is not the visual the team wants. Levels 2–9 will be re-designed to bridge between Lvl 1 and Lvl 10 in a future pass.

## Tier Summary
**Level 10 — Veteran Royal Archer / Knight-Archer.** Fully armored top-tier evolution of the same hooded scout. The character is now a plate-armored ranged knight: silver steel helmet with a gold finial spike, gold-trim helmet band and a fixed full-face visor, silver plate breastplate and shoulder pauldrons, silver vambraces, and a layered red-and-yellow tassets / arming-skirt at the waist that carries the Lvl 1 tunic palette down into the lower body. The wooden recurve bow has been replaced by a **golden recurve bow**, marking the rank visually at the silhouette's primary readable shape. Cape is still crimson red; trousers and boots are unchanged from base.

## Style Inheritance (from `Archer.md`)
- Stylized mobile game character, Supercell / Kingshot lineage, **chibi 2.5–3 heads tall**, hand-painted digital illustration, soft painted shading with visible brushwork, warm top-left key light.
- **Thin black outline** around the full character silhouette in deep warm brown-black, even weight with slight thickening in shadow recesses, slight thinning under key light. Interior linework much softer / thinner, mostly value-driven, never hard black.
- Three-quarter view, full body, clean white background, no environmental context, minimal contact shadow.
- Saturated rich colors, warm shadow tones (never gray/cool on skin or cloth), painted highlights on metal and gems.

## Build & Proportions
Identical to Lvl 1 — same slim athletic chibi rig, ~2.5–3 heads tall, oversized head, slightly enlarged hands, chunky wide-soled feet. The Lvl 10 silhouette reads visibly **stockier** than Lvl 1 because of the layered armor mass at chest, shoulders, and hips, but the underlying skeleton is unchanged.

## Face
The face is **fully concealed by the closed visor**. No skin, hair, or expression is visible at Lvl 10. The visor's horizontal eye slit reads as a dark recessed band; no glow, no eye highlight, no painted eye. (This is a deliberate top-tier design choice — the "veteran soldier" reading comes from the silhouette and palette, not from a personality face.)

If a partial-visor or open-visor variant is ever needed, fall back to the Lvl 1 face description (warm peachy-tan, dark brown brows and hair, warm brown eyes, confident half-smirk).

## Apparel — Helmet
- **Shape**: **Conical / pointed steel helmet** with a slight tapering rise to a small ornament at the apex. Covers the entire head from crown to jaw.
- **Crown finial**: A small **golden spike / teardrop finial** at the very top of the helmet — a clean tapered point with a soft buttery-gold metallic highlight, not chrome-yellow, not glowing.
- **Crown band**: A **horizontal golden trim band** wraps the helmet roughly two-thirds up from the brow, separating the body of the helmet from the upper cone. The band is plain (no engraving) but reads clearly as gold against the silver steel.
- **Visor / face mask**: A **fixed full-face visor** covers the front of the helmet from forehead to chin, with a single **horizontal recessed eye slit** running across at eye level. The visor surface is the same silver steel as the rest of the helmet, painted with a soft top-down highlight and a darker shadow on the lower half. The eye slit is a deep painted-shadow band — almost black inside, no eyes visible.
- **Cheek / jaw plates**: The visor flares slightly outward at the cheekbones and tucks back in at the jaw, giving the helmet a clear chibi-readable face mask shape rather than a featureless dome.
- **Material read**: Polished steel — cool silver-gray base with bright white painted highlights along the upper edge of each plate and warm-brown shadow recesses where the plates meet.
- **Helmet edge / rim**: Where the helmet meets the neck, a slight darker rim line is visible above the red collar.
- **No hood visible at Lvl 10** — the red hood from Lvl 1 has been replaced by the helmet. The red carries through at the neck via the collar / scarf described next.

## Apparel — Neck Collar
- A **red cloth collar / scarf** is visible at the throat between the helmet rim and the breastplate's neckline. It reads as the same crimson-red garment family as the cape (and as the Lvl 1 hood), keeping the red color unbroken from helmet-base down through the cape behind.
- The collar is soft cloth, painted with subtle folds, no trim, no brooch.

## Apparel — Pauldrons (Shoulder Armor)
- **Both shoulders** are covered by **silver steel plate pauldrons**.
- **Shape**: Rounded dome-shaped caps that follow the curve of each shoulder, slightly flaring outward at the bottom edge.
- **Layering**: Each pauldron reads as **two stacked plates** — a smaller upper cap and a larger lower lame, with a clear seam between them and a small painted highlight along the upper plate's leading edge.
- **Rivets**: Three or four small painted rivet dots are visible along the lame seam — clearly drawn but kept subtle, not photorealistic.
- **Material**: Same polished cool silver steel as the helmet, with bright white painted highlights on the dome and warm-brown shadow under the lower lame.
- **Color motif at the inner shoulder**: Where each pauldron meets the body, a **small wedge of red cloth** from the underlying gambeson is visible, tying the red garment family across the silhouette.

## Apparel — Breastplate (Cuirass)
- **Shape**: A single piece **silver steel breastplate** covering the chest from collarbones to mid-torso, fitting the chibi torso closely.
- **Surface**: Painted with a soft top-down gradient — brighter cool silver near the collarbones, slightly darker gray-blue in the lower torso recess, and a clean central highlight running vertically down the chest.
- **Edge / rim**: A subtle darker rim line traces the bottom edge of the breastplate where it meets the tassets / arming skirt below, creating a clean horizontal armor break at the waist.
- **No engraving, no embossed crest, no gem inlay** at Lvl 10 — the gold detailing is reserved for the helmet finial and band, the bow, and the belt buckle. The breastplate is plain polished plate.

## Apparel — Vambraces (Forearm Armor)
- **Both forearms** are covered by **silver steel vambraces** running from wrist to just below the elbow.
- **Shape**: Two-piece tapered tubes, painted with a clear longitudinal seam and a subtle flare at the elbow end.
- **Highlights**: Bright white painted highlight along the leading edge, warm-brown shadow on the inner curve.
- **Hands**: Bare, warm peachy-tan skin, slightly enlarged in the chibi way. **No gloves, no gauntlets** — the vambrace ends at the wrist and the hand is exposed. (This preserves the Lvl 1 "hunter's hands" read; gauntlets would obscure the dexterity-of-the-archer reading.)
- **Bicep / upper arm**: Visible as warm peachy-tan skin between the pauldron and the vambrace — the same sleeveless arm convention from Lvl 1, just framed by metal at top and bottom.

## Apparel — Tassets / Arming Skirt
- The Lvl 1 mustard-yellow tunic with red panels has been **re-articulated as a layered tassets / arming skirt** below the breastplate. This is the most distinctive Lvl 10 design move — the original hero palette of the unit (red + mustard-yellow) survives at hip level.
- **Construction**: A series of **vertical cloth strips / lames** hang from the waistline down to mid-thigh, alternating in color:
  - **Red strips** (matching the cape and collar)
  - **Mustard-yellow strips** (matching the Lvl 1 tunic base)
  - The strips are arranged so the red and yellow alternate around the hip silhouette — visible at the front and on both sides.
- **Material**: Stiffened cloth or quilted fabric, painted with subtle vertical fold lines and a soft warm shadow at the hem.
- **Hem**: Clean cut horizontal hem at mid-thigh; no fringe.
- **Layering**: The skirt sits on top of the trousers and below the wide leather waist belt described next.

## Apparel — Waist Belt
- A **wide warm-brown leather waist belt** wraps the hips at the top of the tassets / arming skirt, sitting just below the lower edge of the breastplate.
- **Buckle**: A **golden / brass rectangular buckle** centered at the front, slightly larger and more prominent than the Lvl 1 belt buckle — reads as a rank-upgrade detail.
- **Material**: Painted leather grain with soft warm highlights and a slight scuff texture; warm-brown like the boots and quiver.
- **Strap end**: A short leather strap-end hangs down from the buckle a few inches over the front of the skirt.

## Apparel — Cape
- **Crimson-red cape**, attached at both shoulders behind the pauldrons, falling to about knee level — same length and weight as Lvl 1.
- **Drape**: Hangs naturally with soft painted vertical folds. On the viewer-left side it curls inward slightly, showing a small wedge of slightly darker red inner lining; on the viewer-right side it falls straighter behind the bow arm.
- **Edge**: Plain rolled hem, no fringe, no gold trim, no embroidery. (At Lvl 10 the gold detailing is concentrated on the helmet, bow, and belt buckle — not the cape, which keeps the cloth color anchor pure.)
- **Inner lining**: Slightly darker shadow-red where the inner curl shows.

## Apparel — Trousers
- Olive / muted forest-green cloth trousers, knee-length, slightly loose at the cuff. **Identical to Lvl 1.**
- Visible between the bottom of the tassets skirt and the top of the boot cuff.

## Apparel — Boots
- Chunky warm-brown leather boots with a darker folded ankle cuff and a wide flat sole. **Identical to Lvl 1.**
- No metal sabatons added at Lvl 10 — the lower legs intentionally remain in the original hunter's silhouette to keep the character recognizable as the same archer line.

## Apparel — Quiver
- Brown leather back-quiver, cylindrical, slung diagonally across the back.
- **Strap**: Brown leather diagonal strap from the anatomical-left shoulder down to the anatomical-right hip; partially obscured by the cape and pauldron but visible where it crosses the upper chest.
- **Body**: Plain warm-brown leather tube with painted darker top and bottom rims; same construction as Lvl 1.
- **Contents**: Three to four arrows visible rising out of the top — **white feather fletching with red bindings / red tips**, pale wood-tan shafts. Identical to Lvl 1.

## Weapon — Bow (Lvl 10 Upgrade)
- **Type**: Recurve short bow — same proportions and silhouette as Lvl 1.
- **Material — limbs**: **Buttery warm gold** (not chrome-yellow) — the entire bow is now metallic / lacquered gold rather than wood-brown. Painted with a soft top-down highlight along each limb and a darker gold shadow on the inner curve. Reads as polished gold metalwork or gold-lacquered wood, not as a glowing magical object.
- **Material — grip**: A **dark brown leather grip wrap** at the central handle, painted with subtle cross-binding texture — the only non-gold element on the bow, providing visual contrast at the grip point.
- **Bowstring**: Thin pale off-white / cream string, drawn as a clean single line with a small painted highlight — same as Lvl 1.
- **Tip caps / nocks**: A small slightly brighter gold highlight at each limb tip; no separate cap color.
- **Held**: Vertically in the **anatomical-left hand** at hip height, gripped at the leather wrap — same pose as all earlier levels.
- **No engraving, no gemstone inlay, no glow** — the gold material itself is the rank statement.

## Pose Description
Three-quarter view, body angled roughly 15–25° to the viewer's left so the character's anatomical right side faces camera. Weight planted evenly on both feet, very slight forward lean. Golden recurve bow held vertically at hip height in the anatomical-left hand (viewer-right side of frame), grip relaxed but ready. The anatomical-right hand (viewer-left side of frame) is held forward across the body at chest / waist height with fingers loosely curled, hovering just above the breastplate — reads as ready to nock and draw. The quiver rises behind the right side of the head with white-fletched arrows clearly silhouetted against the helmet and cape. The cape falls straight behind the body with a soft inward curl on the viewer-left side. The closed visor is the focal point of the silhouette's upper half; the alternating red-and-yellow tassets are the focal point of the lower half.

## Continuity Anchors with Lvl 1
The following Lvl 1 elements **carry through unchanged** and are non-negotiable for keeping the unit identity readable across the progression:
1. **Crimson-red cape** — same color, same length (~knee), same drape, same plain hem.
2. **Red as the dominant cloth color** — at Lvl 1 it lives in the hood + cape + tunic panels; at Lvl 10 it lives in the collar + cape + tassets red strips. Red is always present at the head/neck and at the cape.
3. **Mustard-yellow as the secondary cloth color** — Lvl 1 tunic base; Lvl 10 alternating tassets strips. Yellow is always present somewhere on the torso.
4. **Olive / forest-green trousers** — identical at both levels.
5. **Warm-brown leather boots with darker folded cuff** — identical at both levels.
6. **Brown leather back-quiver with white-feather red-tip arrows** — identical at both levels.
7. **Bow held vertically at hip in the anatomical-left hand**, draw hand forward at chest — identical pose and hand convention. Bow material upgrades from wood (Lvl 1) to gold (Lvl 10) but silhouette is the same.
8. **Warm-brown leather waist belt with a brass / gold rectangular buckle** — same belt family, slightly upgraded buckle at Lvl 10.
9. **Three-quarter view, full body, clean white background, thin black silhouette outline** — style anchors hold across the line.

## Notes for the Lvl 2–9 Re-Design Pass
When we brainstorm the new progression between Lvl 1 (`archer1.png`) and Lvl 10 (this doc), the design ramp should plausibly bridge:

- **Head**: hood (Lvl 1) → hood + light helmet → light helmet only → conical steel helmet → conical steel helmet with closed visor → conical steel helmet with closed visor + gold finial + gold band (Lvl 10). The red cloth must persist somewhere at neck level the whole way.
- **Torso**: sleeveless tunic (Lvl 1) → reinforced tunic / gambeson → leather chest piece → partial steel cuirass → full steel breastplate (Lvl 10).
- **Arms**: bare + bandaged draw hand (Lvl 1) → both bracered → leather vambraces → steel vambraces (Lvl 10). Hands stay bare across the line.
- **Lower body**: plain mustard-and-red tunic hem + olive trousers + brown boots (Lvl 1) → simple cloth tassets that previews the Lvl 10 alternating-strip motif → full red-and-yellow alternating tassets (Lvl 10). Trousers and boots stay unchanged.
- **Shoulders**: bare (Lvl 1) → leather pauldron caps → steel pauldron caps → layered two-plate steel pauldrons with rivets and red-cloth wedge (Lvl 10).
- **Belt**: two-strand brown leather + small brass buckle (Lvl 1) → wide leather belt + bigger brass buckle → wide leather belt + buttery-gold buckle (Lvl 10).
- **Bow**: wood-brown recurve (Lvl 1) → wood-brown recurve with leather grip + small metal end caps → wood-and-gilt mixed recurve → fully gold recurve (Lvl 10). The grip stays dark brown leather throughout.
- **Cape**: crimson-red, knee-length, plain hem — **unchanged across the whole line**. The cape is the strongest continuity anchor.
- **Quiver + arrows + boots + trousers + hand pose + bow-hand convention** — all unchanged across the whole line.

Suggested escalation tempo (to discuss): Lvl 1–2 cloth-only, Lvl 3–4 leather adds, Lvl 5–6 first metal pieces (vambraces + light pauldrons + light helm), Lvl 7–8 full plate torso + closed helm, Lvl 9 plate + tassets first appearance, Lvl 10 gold accents + golden bow as the final rank statement. Open to other tempos.
