# Cavalry — Base Character Spec (L1)

> Canonical L1 source: [Refs/L1_Base.png](Refs/L1_Base.png) — 1024×1024 native (downscaled from a 2048×2048 source backed up at [Refs/L1_Base_2K.png](Refs/L1_Base_2K.png), 2026-05-06).
> All chained generations must preserve the elements below verbatim until a level explicitly upgrades them.

---

## Archetype

EMTD light cavalry / lancer in the same red+yellow+leather livery family as the Archer and Infantry (shared faction palette). **Mounted** on a chibi chestnut-brown horse with a checkered red+blue caparison. The signature visual differentiators from the Archer and Infantry are: **kettle-helm at L1**, **long wooden spear with iron leaf-tip as primary weapon**, **mounted on a real chibi horse with caparison + bridle + reins**, **mustache (no beard)** at L1.

The unit reads as a "young hussar / light lancer" at L1 — kit progresses to a "knight on barded warhorse" by L7, then "royal champion on gold-caparisoned destrier" by L10. The horse is part of the canonical silhouette at every level.

## Pose (canonical L1 stance — to be matched at every level)

Three-quarter view, the rider's body angled approximately 15-25 degrees toward the viewer's left so the rider's anatomical-right side faces camera. The HORSE faces toward the viewer's RIGHT — head and muzzle on the viewer-RIGHT side of the frame, rump and tail on the viewer-LEFT side. Rider sits upright in the saddle with both feet in stirrups, slight forward lean.

- **Spear arm (anatomical-RIGHT, viewer-LEFT)**: bent at the elbow with the upper arm vertical and forearm angled slightly forward. The hand grips the wooden spear haft just above shoulder height; the spear stands roughly VERTICAL with the iron leaf-tip pointing UP toward the top of the canvas. Knuckles of the gripping hand visible to camera.
- **Rein arm (anatomical-LEFT, viewer-RIGHT)**: extended forward and slightly down, elbow bent ~110 degrees, hand gripping the brown leather reins at the level of the horse's withers / shoulder. The rein hand is visible to camera holding the reins in a relaxed grip.

The pose reads as a calm mounted patrol stance — spear shouldered upright but not couched, reins held but not pulled. NOT a charge, NOT a couched-lance attack, NOT a rear / pivot.

## Face

- Chiseled chibi jawline, prominent slightly-hooked nose, thick dark-brown eyebrows with a stern furrow.
- **Thick dark-brown mustache** — full handlebar shape, covering the upper lip and curving down past the corners of the mouth (~½" past each corner). NOT extending into a beard. Cheeks and chin clean-shaven.
- **NO beard at L1.** Chin and cheeks clean-shaven. Beard does NOT arrive in the chain (Cavalry stays mustache-only L1-L8; face concealed by visor L9-L10).
- Mouth set in a flat closed-mouth confident expression (slight upturn at the corners, not a stern furrow).
- Large warm steel-blue eyes with a single bright catchlight in each iris and visible whites of the eyes.
- Warm peachy-tan skin tone, soft rosy blush on the cheeks.
- Dark brown short hair — visible only as a thin fringe at the forehead under the front edge of the kettle-helm.

## Helm

**Steel kettle-helm (already present at L1)** — neutral silver-gray steel matching the L1 kettle-helm tone (balanced gray, neither blue-shadowed nor brown-shadowed) domed helm with a forward-projecting riveted brim. Two visible round iron rivets on each side at the brim seam. Domed crown, gentle sloping forehead curve, brim projects ~1.5" forward over the eyes. Open face — the entire face is fully visible below the brim (mustache, nose, mouth, eyes, cheeks).

The L1 kettle-helm is **steel** (smooth painted steel surface, NOT scale, NOT chainmail). NEVER describe as chainmail / scale-mail / fine ring mesh.

## Torso garment (LIVERY — strongest preserve element)

**Sleeveless yellow + red split surcoat** worn OVER a brown leather long-sleeve under-jacket:

- **Surcoat colors**: viewer-LEFT half of the chest is **mustard yellow**; viewer-RIGHT half of the chest is **crimson red**. Vertical seam runs down the center of the chest. Round neckline. Hem reaches the natural waist (where the brown leather belt sits) — surcoat does NOT extend past the hip into a skirt at L1.
- **Brown leather long-sleeve under-jacket** beneath the surcoat — both arms covered shoulder to wrist in matte warm-brown leather. Soft painted shading, no studs, no armor, no metal at L1.
- **Wide brown leather waist belt** with a square brass buckle centered at the front. Belt sits at the natural waist over the surcoat hem.

**Livery rule** (parallels Archer § Visual Anchors A and Infantry § Part 2 § A): the yellow+red split surcoat is the strongest faction-identity anchor. Must remain visibly dominant on the chest L1-L4. Migrates to chest band L5-L7. Reduces to skirt-only band L8-L9. Disappears under full plate at L10.

## Arms

- **Brown leather long sleeves** at L1 (covering both arms shoulder to wrist).
- Both hands bare warm peachy-tan skin (anatomical-RIGHT grips the spear haft; anatomical-LEFT grips the reins).
- No bracers, no vambraces, no gauntlets at L1.

## Legs / Boots

- **Dark blue / muted steel-blue trousers**, knee-length, slightly loose at the cuff (visible under the saddle skirt on the viewer-LEFT side; viewer-RIGHT side mostly concealed by horse + caparison).
- **Chunky warm-brown leather knee-high riding boots**, mid-calf height, darker folded ankle cuff at the top, wide flat soles. Both boots in stirrups.

## Weapon — wooden spear with iron leaf-tip (anatomical-RIGHT hand)

- Heavy wooden haft, warm-brown finish, ~6 feet long (haft extends from grip level UP past the top of the helm and out of frame at the canvas top edge — spear-tip visible at the top of canvas).
- **Iron leaf-shaped spear-tip** at the top of the haft — broad leaf-shaped neutral-gray steel matching the L1 kettle-helm tone (balanced gray) spearhead, ~8" long, mounted on the wooden haft via a visible iron socket.
- Haft held vertically in the anatomical-RIGHT hand at shoulder level. Spear-tip points UP.
- Weapon evolution rule (LOCKED): the spear/lance is the unit's signature weapon and is **never replaced**. It evolves with armor: wooden spear with iron leaf-tip (L1-L3) → iron-banded haft + larger leaf-tip (L4-L6) → steel lance with pennon (L7-L9) → gilt tournament lance with chased filigree + heraldic pennon (L10). See Progression Plan.

## Reins / horse tack

- **Brown leather bridle** on the horse's head — cheekstraps, browband, throatlash, noseband all matte warm-brown leather, plain at L1 (no metal banding, no studs, no buckles visible).
- **Brown leather reins** running from the bit (concealed in the horse's mouth) back to the rider's anatomical-LEFT hand at the horse's withers.
- **Brown leather saddle** with a small pommel at the front and a low cantle at the back, visible just above the caparison line.

## Horse — chibi chestnut destrier

- **Coat color**: warm chestnut brown with a slightly darker mane and tail. Soft painted shading, warm-brown shadows under the belly and behind the legs.
- **No white markings** — the horse is solid chestnut brown across the entire body, including the muzzle, forehead, legs, hooves, and belly. No white blaze, no white star, no white socks, no white speckling, no white spots. (LOCKED 2026-05-06 after L5 keeper-pick — earlier prompt versions described a "white blaze on the muzzle" which the model was amplifying into broader white markings; corrected to solid-brown.)
- **Dark brown mane** running along the top of the neck, falling to the viewer side at the withers (~3" long, gently brushed forward).
- **Dark brown tail** extending behind the rump, falling to about hock height.
- **Eye**: single dark eye visible on the viewer-RIGHT side of the horse's head (since the horse faces viewer-RIGHT), gentle expression with visible catchlight.
- **Pose**: standing four-square, head facing viewer-RIGHT, neck arched, ears forward. NOT rearing, NOT walking, NOT in motion — calm patrol stance matching the rider's calm mounted stance.
- **Caparison / saddle blanket**: large rectangular cloth caparison draped over the horse's back covering rump + flanks + chest, with a **red+blue checkered pattern** (alternating red and blue squares, ~3" per square) and a **blue trim band** along the lower edge. Drapes to about knee-height on the horse, with the lower edge cut into shallow rectangular tabs.

**Horse evolution rule (LOCKED)**: the horse archetype (solid chestnut color, mane shape, pose) is held verbatim L1-L10. The TACK + BARDING evolves: plain leather bridle (L1) → iron-buckled bridle (L2) → studded leather peytral (L4) → steel chamfron (L5) → full steel chamfron + crinet + flanchards (L7-L9) → gold-trimmed royal barding with rosette chamfron + gold cross emblem on flanchards (L10). The caparison evolves in parallel: checkered red+blue (L1) → red+yellow heraldic with central crest (L7+) → gold-trimmed royal caparison (L10).

## Framing / scale (canonical numbers — LOCKED, measured 2026-05-06)

`Refs/L1_Base.png` is **1024×1024 native** (downscaled from a 2048×2048 source — backup at `Refs/L1_Base_2K.png`). Measured framing numbers (alpha-aware bbox over the rider+horse silhouette):

- Canvas: 1024×1024
- **Vertical fill: 77.64%** (~795px tall — taller than Archer/Infantry because rider+horse stack)
- **Top margin: 11.43%** (~117px — top of rider's helm; spear-tip extends ABOVE the helm and may exit the canvas top)
- **Bottom margin: 10.94%** (~112px — horse's hooves)
- **Horizontal fill: 60.64%** (~621px wide — horse rump on viewer-LEFT to horse muzzle on viewer-RIGHT)
- **Left margin: 21.58%** (~221px)
- **Right margin: 17.77%** (~182px)

These numbers must be reproduced pixel-for-pixel by every chained generation. ±5% drift on vertical fill or top margin is a soft fail; flag and consider re-rolling.

**Per-unit framing envelope is canonical — DO NOT renormalize against Archer (74.7%) or Infantry (66.8%).** Cavalry's 77.64% is the natural rider+horse stack envelope and must be matched at every level. Vertical-extent growth at silhouette-mightiness levels (L7 closed helm, L9 horns, L10 crested helm + plume) is expected and not a fail when concentrated at the new feature.

**Spear-tip note**: the L1 spear extends from grip height UP past the top of canvas — the spear-tip is at or near the top edge. The chain should preserve this — the spear-tip stays in the upper third of the canvas at every level, with the haft reaching to or just above the top edge. Do NOT shrink the spear so the tip falls below the helm.

## Style / outline

Supercell / Kingshot stylized mobile game art, hand-painted digital illustration with soft painted shading and visible brushwork, warm subsurface skin, saturated rich colors, warm shadow tones never gray, soft painted highlights on metal and leather. Clean thin black silhouette outline around the entire rider + horse + spear + caparison silhouette in deep warm brown-black. Clean white background — never describe as transparent.

## Color anchors (held across all 10 levels)

- **Mustard yellow** somewhere on torso/hip (surcoat L1-L4, chest band L5-L7, skirt band L8-L9, gone L10).
- **Crimson red** somewhere on torso/hip — same migration curve as mustard.
- **Cool silver-gray steel** — kettle-helm (L1+), expanding to scale shirt → breastplate → ornate cuirass + closed visor.
- **Warm-brown leather** — under-jacket sleeves, belt, boots, reins, bridle, saddle, spear haft (most leather kit is replaced by metal in plate tier; saddle leather and rein leather hold throughout).
- **Brown wood** — spear haft (held throughout, gets gold end-caps at L10).
- **Iron / steel** — spear leaf-tip (L1+), expanding into iron-banded haft, then gilt tournament lance.
- **Gold** — first appears L8 (pauldron rivets / cuirass cross-emblem accent), expands L9-L9.5, dominant L10 (full gold trim, gold helm crest, gold lance head, gold royal caparison).
- **Dark blue trousers** — held all 10 levels (visible at the calf even when greaves added).
- **Solid chestnut brown horse coat** — held all 10 levels, no white markings of any kind.
- **Red+blue checkered caparison** at L1, evolves to red+yellow heraldic at L7+, gold-trimmed at L10.
