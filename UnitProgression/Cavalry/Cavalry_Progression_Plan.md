# Cavalry — Rank Progression Plan (L1 → L10)

> 10 levels of rank progression for the EMTD Cavalry unit. Same mounted character + same horse, same pose, same framing, progressively richer rider kit + horse barding + visibly mightier silhouette in the plate tiers.
>
> Companion to [Cavalry.md](Cavalry.md) (L1 base spec) and [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) (pipeline + visual learnings).

---

## Locked design rules

- **Weapon archetype = spear / lance — ADDITIVE evolution, wooden haft preserved L1-L10.** Never swapped to mace, sword, axe, bow, or two-handed weapon. The wooden spear haft (warm-brown wood) is preserved across ALL 10 levels with kit accumulating cumulatively: L1 wooden haft + iron leaf-tip; L2 + iron pommel cap at butt; L3 + leather grip cord wrap; L4 + 3 iron banding rings + reinforced socket at leaf-tip; L7 leaf-tip becomes a slightly larger steel leaf head + small red+yellow heraldic pennon flag tied just below the head; L9 + gold trim on pennon; L10 + gold gilt finish on lance head + gold haft end-caps + red+yellow+gold pennon. The HAFT NEVER becomes solid steel. The accumulated kit (pommel cap, grip cord, iron rings, reinforced socket) all remains visible at L10. *(Revised 2026-05-07 per AD FeedbackV0 detail-economy: L5 "steel reinforcement plate" surface item REMOVED; L10 "chased filigree" along the lance flanges REMOVED — gold gilt material change carries the L10 upgrade without filigree; L10 "per-flange-tip spiked finials" REMOVED.)*
- **Mount archetype = real chibi chestnut-brown horse with caparison.** Never swapped to a hobby-horse, war-pony, dire-mount, or any other species. Solid chestnut coat (NO white markings — no blaze, no star, no socks, no speckling) + dark brown mane + dark brown tail + viewer-RIGHT-facing pose locked L1-L10.
- **Livery rule (revised 2026-05-07: chest-band-above-breastplate REMOVED).** Yellow+red split surcoat dominant L1-L4 → at L5 visible as a skirt band below the partial breastplate down to the belt → at L6+ visible only as a SKIRT BAND between the (full) breastplate's bottom edge and the tassets' top edge (~3" at L6-L7, ~2" at L8-L9) → gone at L10. The breastplate sits FLUSH against the bevor from L6 onward — NO cloth visible between the bevor and the breastplate's upper edge (cloth-above-armor pattern looks impractical and was removed per user feedback). The horse caparison carries the heraldic anchor in parallel (red+blue checkered L1-L9.5 → red+yellow + gold cross L10).
- **Silhouette mightiness rule (LOCKED).** Each level in the plate tier (L7-L10) must visibly **increase the rider's silhouette mass and stature** vs the prior level — bulkier pauldrons, taller helm, broader cuirass volume, longer flaring tassets, larger lance head, full horse barding. Don't just swap surface ornaments; bump volumes. The L10 silhouette should clearly read as "bigger and mightier" than the L7 silhouette at thumbnail scale, even before color is registered.
- **Detail Economy rule (LOCKED 2026-05-07 per AD FeedbackV0).** Higher levels gain richness via VOLUME and SILHOUETTE SHAPE, not via stacked surface ornament. Rely on shapes and silhouettes to make the unit feel richer and more elaborate, NOT on sculpted micro-details / engraving / filigree. Cut: chased filigree on the lance head, lion's-head pauldron emboss, gilt per-edge piping on cuirass/tassets/chamfron, gauntlet per-knuckle gold plates, gold rivets on pauldrons, embossed gold studs on shield/saddle, micro-rivet runs, heraldic stamps on flanchard corners. Keep: volume bumps (taller helm, wider pauldrons, fewer-but-wider tassets, longer/flaring skirt edges, bigger lance head, full horse barding extents), material changes (wood → iron → steel → gold).
- **Belt-kit minimalism (LOCKED 2026-05-07 per AD FeedbackV0).** Hip pouch at L2 (spear-side hip) is the ONLY belt accessory carried through the chain. **Knife / dagger sheath REMOVED entirely** (was at L3 — dropped per AD "remove the dagger entirely and keep the pouch"). **Iron belt studs REMOVED entirely** (was 8 round-head studs at L2 — dropped 2026-05-07 per AD detail-economy "embossed studs ... use clean wider band instead" first-cut item; would have persisted through every later level as permanent surface ornament). No additional pouches, no tool-loops, no tags, no decorative metalwork on the belt. The belt remains plain warm-brown leather with the existing brass buckle only.
- **Boot armor split (LOCKED 2026-05-07 per AD FeedbackV0).** AD: "The armored pieces on the boots could also be split into smaller upgrades and distributed earlier." Split: iron toe-cap strip at **L4** → ankle plates at **L6** → full sabatons at **L8**. Three smaller adds instead of one big L6 sabaton step.
- **Full-arm scale-mail at later levels (LOCKED 2026-05-07 per AD FeedbackV0).** AD: "The chainmail being sleeveless feels odd. For the later levels, I would extend it to fully cover the arms." Scale-mail sleeves grow from short (mid-bicep, L4) → full bicep-to-wrist (L5+) sitting OVER the brown leather under-jacket sleeves; from L5 onward the steel vambraces sit ON TOP of the scale-mail at the forearm, no exposed leather-jacket gap on the upper arm.
- **Pose locked.** L1 stance held verbatim L1-L10 (spear vertical anatomical-RIGHT hand, reins gripped anatomical-LEFT hand at horse withers, three-quarter view, horse facing viewer-RIGHT). Framing locked except possibly L10 if the tall gold crest spike needs more headroom (decide at plan-test time).
- **Chained composite-only pipeline.** Every level uses Rule-2 chained input (prior composited keeper + L1_Base scale anchor). No Rule-1 anchored fallback prompts at tier breaks. **Composite + denoise is the mandatory drift-mitigation step between every level lock and the next chain step** — see CLAUDE.md § Pipeline. Tier-break drift mitigation is delegated to the composite step at locked low/high `--dilate 15-20`, not to a fresh-from-L1 anchored prompt.
- **Mustache held L1-L8** (handlebar shape, no beard). Face concealed by closed visor at L9-L10.

## Tier structure

| Tier | Levels | Theme | Silhouette read |
|------|--------|-------|-----------------|
| **Tier 1 — Light Lancer** | L1 — L3 | Cloth + leather + kettle helm. Wooden spear. Plain horse tack. | Compact mounted scout, kettle-hooded. Same envelope as L1. |
| **Tier 2 — Hussar** | L4 — L6 | Iron-banded + scale shirt + reinforced spear + studded horse barding. | Slightly bulkier — armored sleeves, banded haft, leather peytral on horse. |
| **Tier 3 — Knight** | L7 — L9 | Plate cuirass + closed helm + steel lance with pennon + steel chamfron + crinet on horse. | Visibly larger — broad pauldrons, taller helm, plate volume on rider, articulated steel on horse head and neck. |
| **Royal — Champion** | L9.5 — L10 | Gold-trimmed plate + gold-crested helm + ornate gilt lance + full plate horse barding with gold caparison. | Mightiest — flaring tassets, gold helm crest extends silhouette upward, horse fully armored + gold-caparisoned. |

L4 and L7 are **tier-break levels** — large delta. Composite step uses `--dilate 15` (or 20 for L7's plate cuirass + chamfron + steel lance combo).

## Vocabulary risks (Cavalry-specific)

- **Spear** — say "long wooden spear with iron leaf-tip" (L1) / "iron-banded wooden spear with reinforced socket" (L4) / "steel lance with red+yellow pennon" (L7+) / "gilt-flanged ornate lance with heraldic pennon" (L10). Never just "spear" — model defaults to a generic short javelin shape.
- **Lance** — same caveat as spear at higher tiers; always describe head shape + material + pennon presence.
- **Mounted** — say "mounted on a chibi solid-chestnut-brown horse with NO white markings" — never just "on a horse" (model often shrinks the horse or swaps to a pony / donkey). Never describe a "white blaze" or any white feature on the horse — the model amplifies it (white blaze locked-out 2026-05-06).
- **Caparison** — model often misreads as "drape" or "blanket" and renders generically. Always describe the pattern explicitly (red+blue checkered L1, red+yellow heraldic L7+, gold-trimmed royal L10).
- **Mustache** — say "thick dark-brown handlebar mustache, no beard, clean-shaven cheeks and chin" — never just "mustache" (model frequently grows it into a beard, especially at higher armor tiers).
- **Sallet** vs **kettle helm** vs **closed helm** — at L7 the L7 helm is "open-face steel sallet helm with a forward-projecting brim and a short backswept tail" (NOT kettle helm — kettle's brim is wider all-around).
- **Chainmail** — model renders fine ring mesh; always "scale mail" + chunky overlapping scale descriptive (same as Archer/Infantry).
- **Transparent** background — emits checkerboard pattern in RGB; "clean white background" only.
- **Hobby-horse / wooden cutout horse** — NEVER. The Cavalry mount is a real chibi horse with proper anatomy (legs, ears, eye, mane, tail, hooves, neck arch). The Kingshot reference's hobby-horse stylization is NOT used in EMTD Cavalry.
