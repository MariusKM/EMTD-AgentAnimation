# Animation Reference: Tax Collector (Moneybags)

> Source: `tax-collector-aac-audio.mov` | 30 FPS | 211 frames | 7.0s duration

## Character
The **Moneybags** character (referred to as "Tax Collector" in this animation). Dark red beret with white feather, navy blue coat with gold zigzag trim, crimson undershirt, gold wrist cuffs, ornate gold-plated flintlock pistol, brown money pouch. Matches the canonical hero art closely.

## Rendering Style
- **3D-rendered** at **30 FPS** (higher than the other 24 FPS references — notably smoother)
- **Black background** (not transparent)
- **Soft blue rim light** on character edges — intentional, for compositing onto the blue in-game background
- Warm key light from upper-left
- No ground shadow
- This is the **largest file** (114 MB) and highest frame-rate animation in the set

## Animation Breakdown

### Phase 1: Swagger Pose (0.0s - 1.0s)
- Moneybags stands in a confident wide stance, pistol raised high in right hand, money pouch in left hand at hip
- Toothy devious grin, gold tooth visible
- White feather on beret has gentle sway
- Body language radiates cocky self-assurance

### Phase 2: Power Movement — Pistol Flourish + Coin Shower (1.0s - 4.5s)
- **Pistol aim** (~1.0s): Pistol sweeps down and forward, aimed at the viewer. Open-mouth laughing expression — he's enjoying this.
- **Coin toss begins** (~2.0s): Left hand squeezes the money pouch and coins begin erupting upward — **multiple gold coins** fly into the air as VFX particles
- **Full coin shower** (~2.5-3.0s): Coins arc upward and begin raining down around the character. Pistol fully extended forward at arm's length. The coin shower is the **primary VFX element** — 6-8 individually animated gold coins tumbling and spinning in the air.
- **Scattered coins peak** (~3.0-3.5s): Maximum coin spread — coins visible at multiple heights around the character, some above his head, some at his sides, some falling toward the ground
- **Arms wide open** (~3.5-4.0s): Both arms spread wide — pistol in right hand pointed outward, empty money pouch in left. Full frontal stance, coins still cascading. This is the **hero moment** — maximum dramatic spread.
- **Coins landing** (~4.0-4.5s): Coins settle to ground level, some resting on the "floor." Pistol raised high triumphantly, head tilted back with a laugh.

### Phase 3: Coins on Ground + Recovery (4.5s - 6.0s)
- Gold coins visible scattered on the ground around his feet
- Body begins to pull back from the spread-arm stance
- Pistol returns toward raised position
- Expression shifts from laughing back to the knowing smirk
- Ground coins begin to fade/disappear

### Phase 4: Return to Idle (6.0s - 7.0s)
- Full return to the original swagger pose
- All VFX coins have disappeared
- Money pouch back in left hand
- Seamless loop

## Key Motion Observations

### Timing & Easing
- **Theatrically paced** — movements are big, deliberate, and showy
- The coin toss has a **snappy trigger** (quick squeeze) followed by a **slow-motion scatter** quality
- Arm spread is **slow and triumphant** — holding the pose for effect
- This is the most **performance-oriented** timing in the reference set
- The 30 FPS makes the motion noticeably smoother than the 24 FPS animations

### VFX: Coin Particles
- **Gold coins** are individually modeled/animated, not generic particle effects
- Each coin **tumbles and spins** independently with its own rotation
- Coins follow **natural parabolic arcs** — thrown upward, arc, fall with gravity
- Coins at rest on the ground have **flat resting poses** (not floating)
- This is the most **complex VFX element** in any of the reference animations
- The coins serve as both power-display and personality expression (greed/showmanship)

### Secondary Motion
- **White feather**: Prominent sway and bounce during body movements — very visible against the dark beret
- **Coat flaps**: Navy coat panels swing during the arm spread, showing crimson lining
- **Gold wrist cuffs**: Catch light during arm movements, creating glinting effects
- **Money pouch**: Visible emptying/deflating during the coin toss
- **Beret**: Slight shift on head during dramatic head tilts

### Character Consistency
- **Very consistent** throughout — the most detailed character with the most accessories, and all hold up well
- The gold tooth is visible in multiple frames during the laugh
- Color palette (navy, crimson, gold) remains vibrant and stable

## Quality Assessment
- **Highest production value** in the reference set — smoothest motion, most VFX, longest duration
- The coin shower is a **standout VFX element** that demonstrates the level of visual spectacle expected for hero animations
- Personality is expressed through both motion and VFX — the showmanship of scattering coins is pure character
- The 30 FPS provides notably smoother motion — worth considering as a target frame rate
- Demonstrates that hero animations can (and should) include **interactive VFX elements** specific to each character's identity

## Key Takeaway for Pipeline
This sets the VFX bar for hero animations. Each hero should have a **character-specific VFX element** during their power movement — it's not just about the weapon action, but about a visual spectacle that expresses personality. The coin shower is to Moneybags what slash trails are to Diana — a signature effect.
