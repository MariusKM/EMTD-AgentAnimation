# Animation Reference: Sneak / Spy

> Source: `sneak-with-aac-audio.mov` | 24 FPS | 169 frames | 7.0s duration

## Character
The **Spy** character — hooded rogue in dark brown cloak, crossbow, arrow quiver on back, leather satchel. Matches the canonical Spy hero art. The longest animation in the current reference set.

## Rendering Style
- **3D-rendered** in the Supercell/Kingshot stylized aesthetic
- **Black background** (not transparent)
- **Soft blue rim/edge light** visible on silhouette — intentional, for compositing onto the blue in-game background
- Warm key light from upper-left
- No ground shadow visible

## Animation Breakdown

### Phase 1: Sneaking Idle (0.0s - 1.0s)
- Spy in a **hunched, crouching posture** — knees bent, body leaning forward
- Crossbow held at waist level, both hands on it, loading a bolt
- Devious grin visible, eyes narrowed — perfectly in character
- Subtle **side-to-side scanning** motion — looking around suspiciously
- This idle is distinctly different from the other characters — the spy never fully stands upright

### Phase 2: Power Movement — Crossbow Load and Aim (1.0s - 4.0s)
- **Loading** (~1.0-1.5s): Hands manipulate the crossbow, pulling back to load a bolt. Body leans forward slightly.
- **Rising aim** (~1.5-2.0s): Spy straightens up somewhat (still crouched) and extends the crossbow forward, transitioning to a wider aiming stance
- **Full aim extension** (~2.0-3.0s): Crossbow fully extended at arm's length, aimed directly forward. The spy's body is turned more toward the viewer — a wider, more aggressive stance than the sneaking idle. Legs spread for stability.
- **Turning aim** (~3.0-3.5s): Body rotates, aiming shifts from right to more center/left — scanning for targets
- **Hold and fire implied** (~3.5-4.0s): Crossbow held at full extension, slight recoil-like motion

### Phase 3: Sneaking Glance / Return (4.0s - 5.5s)
- Spy begins to pull crossbow back toward body
- **Looking around** (~5.0s): Head turns noticeably to the side — a suspicious glance, checking if anyone saw
- The facial expression shifts from aggressive focus to sly satisfaction
- Body lowers back into the more hunched position

### Phase 4: Return to Idle (5.5s - 7.0s)
- Full return to the original crouching/loading pose
- Crossbow pulled back to chest level
- Resume the sneaking idle stance
- Seamless loop connection

## Key Motion Observations

### Timing & Easing
- **Deliberately slower and more deliberate** than Diana or the knight — sneaking movement, not combat
- The aiming phase has a **steady, controlled quality** — no sudden jerks
- The suspicious glance is **quick** — a sharp head turn that reads clearly
- Overall pacing feels **patient and methodical** — befitting a stealthy character
- At 7 seconds, this is the **longest loop** — the extra time gives space for the personality beats (glancing, loading)

### Personality in Motion
- This animation has the **strongest personality expression** of the five references
- The character is always "acting" — looking suspicious, grinning, glancing around
- The facial expression **changes throughout**: devious loading grin → focused aiming → satisfied smirk → suspicious glance
- This demonstrates that hero animations should carry **emotional arcs**, not just mechanical weapon actions

### Secondary Motion
- **Hood**: Gentle sway and settling when the body shifts, slightly loose fabric movement
- **Quiver/bolts**: Arrows shift slightly in the quiver during body rotation — subtle but present
- **Satchel**: Leather pouch at hip swings during lateral movement
- **Crossbow string**: Visible tension change during the loading/aiming sequence
- **Cloak edges**: Bottom hem of the cloak has gentle drift

### Character Consistency
- **Very consistent** design throughout — the 3D model holds the 2D style well
- The large pointed nose (Spy's signature feature) is clearly visible from all angles
- Hood draping remains natural through all poses

## Quality Assessment
- **Excellent animation** — the best personality work in the reference set
- The narrative arc (sneak → load → aim → fire → check if safe) tells a mini-story
- Facial expression changes add significant life and personality
- The patient timing is perfect for this character type
- Most valuable reference for understanding how **personality should drive timing and motion choices**

## Key Takeaway for Pipeline
This is the gold standard for personality-driven hero animation. Every character should have this level of "acting" — their animation should tell a mini-story that reveals who they are, not just demonstrate their weapon. The emotional arc is as important as the physical motion.
