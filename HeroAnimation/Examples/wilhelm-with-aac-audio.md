# Animation Reference: Count Wilhelm

> Source: `wilhelm-with-aac-audio.mov` | 24 FPS | 121 frames | 5.0s duration

## Character
**Count Wilhelm** — white-haired veteran warlord with large white mustache, silver plate armor, red cape, ornate battle axe with etched scrollwork, gold gem necklace with red/orange stones. Matches the canonical hero art. The shortest animation in the reference set.

## Rendering Style
- **3D-rendered** in the Supercell/Kingshot stylized aesthetic
- **Black background** (not transparent)
- **Soft blue rim light** on character edges — intentional, for compositing onto the blue in-game background
- Warm key light from upper-left
- No ground shadow

## Animation Breakdown

### Phase 1: Battle-Ready Stance (0.0s - 1.0s)
- Wilhelm stands in a wide, grounded stance holding the battle axe diagonally with both hands
- Axe head on the left side, haft crossing the body
- Stern, intimidating expression — furrowed brow, deep scowl
- Red cape drapes behind, slight ambient drift
- Gem necklace visible at chest — gold settings with red/orange stones

### Phase 2: Power Movement — Axe Overhead Slam (1.0s - 3.5s)
- **Wind-up** (~1.0-1.5s): Axe rises upward — right hand pulls the axe up and to the right. Body begins to coil. The axe head passes above head height.
- **Overhead raise** (~1.5-2.0s): Both arms bring the axe fully overhead, behind the head. Body leans back slightly — maximum anticipation. The axe head is now at the highest point, directly above/behind Wilhelm. Cape billows outward from the body rotation.
- **Forward thrust/slam** (~2.0-2.5s): Axe swings forward and downward in a massive overhead chop. The axe blade comes **extremely close to camera/viewer** — dramatic perspective foreshortening with the blade appearing very large in the frame. This is the **impact frame**.
- **Low recovery** (~2.5-3.0s): Axe continues past the bottom of the swing, held low at extended arm's length to the right side. Body in a deep, wide-legged stance from the force of the swing.
- **Return arc** (~3.0-3.5s): Axe sweeps back through a low arc — returning from the right side back through center

### Phase 3: Return to Idle (3.5s - 5.0s)
- Axe returns to the diagonal two-handed grip across the body
- Body straightens back to the battle-ready stance
- Cape settles from the motion
- Expression returns to the stern resting scowl
- Seamless loop

## Key Motion Observations

### Timing & Easing
- **Heaviest, most powerful motion** in the reference set — this is the weightiest character
- The wind-up is **slow and deliberate** (~1s of anticipation) — you feel the axe's weight being lifted
- The downward slam is **fast and forceful** — maximum velocity at the impact point
- The extreme contrast between slow wind-up and fast slam creates a **powerful impact sensation**
- At 5 seconds, this is the **shortest loop** — the motion is efficient and impactful, no wasted time

### Perspective & Camera Feel
- The axe slam uses **dramatic foreshortening** — the blade grows very large as it swings toward the camera
- This creates an **almost 3D/parallax effect** even in the static camera setup
- The foreshortening on the axe head at ~2.0s is the most dramatic perspective work in any reference
- This technique should be noted as an option for other heroes — using depth/perspective to add impact

### Secondary Motion
- **Red cape**: The most dynamic cape in the references — billows outward during the overhead raise, wraps around during the slam, settles slowly during recovery. The cape is doing **heavy lifting** for conveying the force of the swing.
- **White mustache**: Subtle bounce during the slam impact
- **Gem necklace**: Stones catch light during the upward swing, visible swinging during rapid motion
- **Hair**: White hair follows head movement with delay
- **Armor plates**: Pauldrons and chest plate shift subtly during the extreme body rotation

### Character Consistency
- Design remains **very solid** even during the extreme overhead pose where the character is viewed from an unusual angle
- The axe scrollwork design is consistently visible
- Armor materials (silver metal, teal undergarment) read clearly throughout

## Quality Assessment
- **Excellent power animation** — the most physically impactful motion in the reference set
- The slow-anticipation-to-fast-strike pattern is textbook animation principle, executed perfectly
- Cape physics are the best in any reference — genuinely reactive to the body motion
- The foreshortening technique adds cinematic depth to a static-camera setup
- At only 5 seconds, it's efficiently paced — the shorter loop works because the action is decisive and authoritative, matching Wilhelm's personality

## Key Takeaway for Pipeline
This demonstrates how **weapon weight should drive timing**. Heavy weapons (axe, hammer) need long wind-ups and explosive releases. Light weapons (Diana's sword) get faster, snappier arcs. The foreshortening technique (weapon swinging toward the camera) is a powerful tool for adding depth and impact. Cape physics should scale with action intensity.
