## Role
The military commander — disciplined, no-nonsense, fully armored. A soldier through and through, defined by duty and readiness.

## Physical Description
- **Build**: Stocky and sturdy, fully encased in armor — the most heavily armored character
- **Skin**: Tan with warm undertones, visible only on face
- **Hair**: Not visible (covered by helmet), facial stubble suggests dark brown
- **Facial hair**: Full dark beard covering jaw and chin, neatly trimmed
- **Expression**: Stern and stoic — set jaw, slight frown, focused intensity
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, narrow and serious, shadowed under the helmet brim

## Costume & Equipment
- **Helmet**: Full medieval knight's sallet/bascinet with a prominent red feathered plume/crest on top. Silver polished metal with visible rivets.
- **Armor**: Full plate silver armor — pauldrons, breastplate, arm plates, leg plates. The most complete armor set in the roster.
- **Cape**: Dark gray/charcoal cape draped from shoulders
- **Under-armor**: Visible dark fabric/chainmail at joints
- **Boots**: Brown leather boots under leg armor
- **Gauntlets**: Brown leather gauntlets at the hands, visible at the sword grip
- **Weapon**: Short straight sword — simple, functional, military-issue. Held point-down with both hands resting on the pommel in a sentinel stance.

## Pose Description
Front-facing three-quarter view, standing upright in a sentinel/guard stance. Sword held point-down in front of the body, both hands on the pommel. Posture rigid and disciplined — the military professional.

**Hand convention (anatomical, not viewer-side)**: both hands rest on the pommel of the sword, with the **right hand on top** (dominant) and the left hand below. For any power-move beat that transitions to a single-hand grip (raise, thrust, salute), the **anatomical right hand** is the active sword-arm and the left hand releases to the side or back to the hilt afterwards. Use the term "right hand" / "left hand" in prompts in this anatomical sense throughout.

## Weapon
- **Primary**: Short straight sword (military-issue) — held point-down in a sentinel/guard stance, both hands resting on the pommel.
- **Natural one-beat action**: Raise sword from guard stance → one clean forward thrust or crisp horizontal slash → return to guard. Alternative: draw/unsheathe motion flowing into a confident salute with the blade. Big strides of confidence are his walking style; for the seated hero screen beat, translate that into a decisive, controlled sword action.
- **Power move direction**: Discipline and precision. Slower and more powerful than Diana — a single deliberate strike, no flourish. Tight blade trail VFX on the slash (sharper/cleaner than Diana's wide arc). A dry stoic smirk is the comedy beat — keep it minimal; his personality sells through weight and restraint, not expression acrobatics.
- **Source of truth**: CSV `Default Attack Style` — "Medium attack speed, medium melee range. Stabs and slashes with sword — slower but more powerful than Diana." Silviu notes — "Melee support unit that grants powerful buffs to nearby allies" (ability kit — ignore for power move).
