# Dragon

> Source: `Source/Heroes Stylized/Dragon.png`
> **Animation scope: IDLE ONLY.** End-game / heavy-tier unit, but still rank-and-file in the army sense — seen on the army screen, not as a named hero. Loop is idle in the same family as the hero breathing-baseline + expressive variants, scaled for unit context. **Tonal register (AD direction 2026-05-11): juvenile / teenager / rebellious dragon — still menacing, but with a comedic edge.** Permitted small comedic beats include an **accidental small fire puff, a small burp with a tiny ember, or a small cough with a soft soot puff** — NOT a deliberate fire-breath, NOT a roar, NOT a wing-spread, NOT a take-off, NOT large flames.
> No corresponding `UnitProgression/Dragon/` progression spec exists yet — this doc establishes the canonical L1 visual description for the idle-loop pipeline. (Update the cross-reference if a rank-progression pipeline is later scaffolded.)

## Role
End-game heavy unit — a stylized chibi red dragon mascot in the EMTD Supercell / Kingshot lineage. **Tonally a juvenile / teenager dragon** with a rebellious streak — still menacing enough to read as a heavy unit silhouette in the army roster, but carries a comedic teenage attitude (knowing smirk, attitude-laden side-glance, the occasional accidental burp or small fire puff that gives away the fact that his fire-breathing isn't fully under control yet). The personality is "menacing rebellious teen with comedic accidents," NOT "rampaging beast" and NOT "proud mythic mascot." Sits as a hero-tier silhouette in the army roster despite being a unit.

## Physical Description
- **Build**: Chibi quadruped dragon — oversized head (~30–35% of total figure height), thick neck tapering into a stocky barrel torso, short stubby legs sitting on its haunches, long flexible tail curling around behind the body. Two folded wings rising up and back from the shoulders. ~3 heads tall when seated (including the wings, the silhouette is taller).
- **Head**: Wedge-shaped chibi dragon head — slightly upturned snout, large rounded jaw, prominent rounded cheek-frill plates (small flared scales fanning out behind each cheek/jaw). Two prominent backswept horns rising from the top of the skull, curving back and slightly outward, with a dark warm-brown to black tip. Smaller backswept "ear" spikes / hornlets along the sides of the skull behind the main horns. A small triangular nose ridge at the top of the snout.
- **Eyes**: **Bright green** with a black pupil, large and alert with a clear catchlight. Almond / cat-like shape with a slight upward angle at the outer corners — gives a knowing, slightly mischievous read.
- **Brow ridges**: Pronounced — small spiked scale plates along the upper edge of each brow, giving the eyes a focused, slightly fierce framing.
- **Mouth**: Closed in the **canonical resting pose**, lips meeting in a subtle confident half-smirk shape with the corners turned slightly up — knowing teenage attitude, not snarling. **Permitted to open briefly mid-loop** for one of the AD-approved small comedic beats (accidental small fire puff, small burp with ember, small cough with soot puff). Always returns to the canonical closed half-smirk by loop end (FFLF integrity).
- **Teeth / fangs**: Not visible in the canonical closed-mouth pose. If the mouth opens briefly mid-loop for a comedic puff / burp / cough, two small upper fangs and a row of small lower teeth become briefly visible — kept chibi-cute (rounded, not razor-sharp), and the mouth returns to closed before loop end.
- **Scales**: Painted hand-rendered scales across the dorsal surfaces (head crown, neck, shoulders, back, outer wings, flanks, tail, outer legs). Each scale a small rounded leaf-shape, arranged in overlapping horizontal rows. The texture reads as **chunky, hand-painted, clearly individual scales** at character-card scale — NOT fine fish-scale, NOT pixel-dot, NOT smooth.
- **Underbelly**: Smooth **warm gold / honey-yellow** underbelly running from the lower jaw down the throat, chest, belly, and inner tail. Painted as overlapping wide flat plate-segments (like a snake or crocodile's ventral scutes) — visible horizontal segmentation from throat to tail. The underbelly color is clearly distinct from the dorsal red.
- **Spines / dorsal ridge**: Row of small triangular dorsal spines running from the back of the skull, down the neck, along the spine, and down to the tip of the tail. Same warm brown-black tip color as the horns.
- **Tail**: Long flexible tail curling around behind/beside the body to about hip-height. Tip ends in a **dragon-leaf spade shape** — two small triangular fins flanking a central pointed tip, in the same warm gold underbelly color on the inner face and dorsal red on the outer face.
- **Claws**: Each foot has four claws — three forward + one rear (dewclaw). Cool black-brown claws painted with a soft highlight.
- **Wings**: Two leathery membrane wings rising up and back from the shoulders. Wing arm (humerus + forearm + finger digits) in dorsal red with chunky scales; wing membrane stretched between the digits in a deeper, slightly desaturated red with subtle warm shadow showing the thinness of the membrane. Outer wing edge has small dorsal spines mirroring the back ridge. Wings are folded but slightly raised — the silhouette reads as "alert and ready to extend," not "fully tucked away."

## Pose Description
Three-quarter view, body angled ~15–25° to the viewer's left so the dragon's anatomical right side faces camera. **Seated on its haunches** with both rear legs folded under, rear paws planted; both front legs/feet planted on the ground in front, supporting the upright posture. Chest puffed slightly, neck arched in a relaxed S-curve, head held high with a slightly cocked teenage attitude, gaze level with the camera or angled in a knowing side-glance.

**Wings**: folded but slightly raised behind the shoulders — not flat against the back, not fully extended. Wing tips reach to about head-height on the silhouette. Outer wing arms curve up and back from the shoulders.

**Tail**: long flexible tail curls behind the body to the viewer-RIGHT side, looping around the rear haunches with the spade tip resting near or just touching the ground beside the rear paw on the viewer-right.

**Head**: held high and forward, mouth closed in a subtle confident half-smirk. Eyes alert, looking slightly toward the camera with the green iris and catchlight clearly readable. Horns sweep back and slightly outward, framing the upper silhouette.

Reads as a **rebellious-teen sitting stance** — alert, slightly menacing, knowing, attitudinal, NOT a roar, NOT a deliberate fire-breath, NOT a wing-flap, NOT a take-off. The smirk and side-glance carry the teenage register; the bulk and silhouette carry the menace.

## Weapon
- **Primary**: None — the dragon's natural weapons are its **claws** (front + rear), **fangs**, **horns**, and **fire-breath**. Mentioned for completeness, but **none of these activate in the idle loop**.
- **Idle scope note**: claws stay planted, mouth defaults closed (opens briefly mid-loop only for the AD-approved comedic beat per Animation Direction), wings stay folded-but-raised, tail stays curled. No combat beat, no deliberate fire-breath weapon.

## Animation Direction
**Juvenile rebellious-teen idle with optional small comedic accident beats.** Continuous ambient breathing as the underlying rhythm — chest puffs gently and almost imperceptibly under the gold underbelly plates, shoulders rise and fall with breath, wings drift / settle subtly with the breath rhythm (not a flap — just a hair of secondary motion at the wing tips), tail tip twitches almost imperceptibly, dorsal spines along the neck and back catch a faint shimmer of painted highlight with the breath.

On top of the breathing baseline, the dragon carries a **teenage attitude register** — head tilted slightly, eyes tracking off-camera with a knowing side-glance, occasional bored cat-like tail-tip swish, occasional small ear-spike twitch. The mouth holds a **knowing smirk** rather than a regal calm-confident expression — the attitude reads as "yeah, what?" not "I am dignified."

Permitted comedic beats (AD direction 2026-05-11 — pick at most ONE per variant, NEVER combined):
- **Accidental small fire puff** — the chest hitches in a small involuntary breath, the mouth opens briefly, a tiny short-lived chibi flame puff escapes the mouth and dissipates within a fraction of a second, the dragon's eyes widen for a beat in a surprised / sheepish "oh" read, then the mouth closes back to smirk. NOT a flame stream, NOT a sustained breath, NOT a deliberate fire-breath — just a small puff he didn't mean to do.
- **Small burp with a tiny ember** — the chest hitches in a small hiccup, the mouth opens briefly, a small ember puff rises and dissipates within a fraction of a second, the dragon's expression reads slightly embarrassed / smug-recovery, then the mouth closes back to smirk.
- **Small cough with a soft soot puff** — the chest hitches in a small involuntary cough, a soft dark soot / smoke puff escapes the briefly-open mouth and dissipates quickly, the dragon recovers with a small head-shake and the mouth closes back to smirk.

**Additional deliberate variant (AD direction extension 2026-05-11, user-approved):**
- **Smug show-off fire-breath** — the dragon arches his neck and chin back in a confident proud lift, the mouth opens with a smug-knowing look, and he releases a **controlled short chibi cartoon flame demonstration** forward — warm orange-yellow-red, hand-painted cartoon flame shape, lasting roughly 0.8–1.2s and reaching no more than a snout-length or two beyond the mouth. The flame trails off; the mouth closes back to canonical smug smirk with a chin-down "yeah, I did that" attitude beat. This is the **only permitted deliberate fire-breath variant** — strictly cartoon-scale flex, never a sustained breath weapon, never a flame stream, never a war-flame. Reads as a teenager demonstrating his ability to flex, not as a hero-tier fire-breath power move.

Default option remains a **breathing-only variant** with no comedic or show-off beat at all (the safe baseline the team can always pick). Expressive idles propose the comedic-accident and show-off beats above as additional variants alongside the breathing baseline, NEVER instead of it.

**Critical idle constraints**:
- The mouth defaults **CLOSED in a smirk**; opens briefly mid-loop ONLY for the AD-approved comedic beat OR the deliberate show-off beat above, returning to closed by loop end (**FFLF integrity** — first and last frame match canonical closed-smirk pose).
- The wings stay **FOLDED** — no flap, no extend, no flutter beyond ambient drift.
- The dragon stays **SEATED** — no rear, no stand, no step, no take-off.
- The head stays in the relaxed S-curve neck position — no full snake-coil neck twist.
- Comedic-accident fire/smoke is **TINY and SHORT-LIVED** — never a flame stream, never a sustained breath, never large flames, never reaches beyond an inch or two from the snout.
- The deliberate show-off fire-breath is **CARTOON-SCALE and BOUNDED** — controlled short flame demonstration reaching no more than a snout-length or two beyond the mouth, never a sustained breath weapon, never a flame stream, never a war-flame.
- NO roar, NO snarl, NO growl, NO hero-tier fire-breath, NO war-flame, NO sustained flame stream.

**Vocal direction**: no actual words, no actual speech, no music, **no roar, no growl, no snarl**. Audio is soft natural ambient deep slow breathing (deeper than human — this is a large creature) + a faint leathery rustle from the folded wing membranes + soft scale-creak from the dorsal plates with the breath. On comedic beats, layer in a small audible event at the moment of the beat — a soft small puff for the fire spit, a small soft burp-hiccup for the burp, a small soft cough-puff for the cough. (Use `no actual words no actual speech no music` framing, not `no voice` — see HeroAnimation CLAUDE.md silence-collapse lesson. Add `no roar no growl no snarl no dragon vocalization no deliberate fire-breath` as an additional explicit ban — the audio model will reach for cliché roar SFX otherwise.)

## Visual Anchors — Critical Vocabulary
- **`dragon` alone** leans Western adult-dragon (long, lithe, fanged, fierce) — always say `chibi cartoon dragon with oversized head and stocky barrel torso in the Supercell / Kingshot mobile game style`.
- **`scales` alone** over-reads to fine fish-scale — always say `chunky hand-painted overlapping rounded leaf-shaped scales`, same family as the Infantry mail-coif vocabulary risk.
- **`fire-breath` / `flame stream` / `breathing fire` / `breath weapon` (deliberate, sustained)** — DO NOT use these phrasings; the keyword anchor pulls large sustained fire VFX into the latent even when negated. The AD-approved comedic beats (small accidental puff / burp ember / cough soot) are described with **tightly scoped language** instead: `a tiny short-lived chibi flame puff escapes briefly from his mouth, a small puff no larger than his snout, dissipates within a fraction of a second, not a flame stream not a sustained breath not a deliberate fire-breath`. Anchor with `puff` (not `breath`), `tiny` / `small` / `short-lived` (not `large` / `long`), and explicit negation of streams / sustained burns / deliberate fire-breath. For breathing-only variants, DO NOT mention fire / flame / smoke anywhere (positive OR negative) — same as the prior rule.
- **`wings spread` / `wings extended`** — never use; always `folded but slightly raised wings`. The model jumps to wide-spread silhouettes on any open-wing language.
- **`horns`** — describe positively as `two prominent backswept horns from the top of the skull, dark warm-brown with near-black tips` to prevent ram-horn / spiral-horn / bull-horn over-reads.
- **`gold underbelly`** — describe as `warm honey-gold underbelly with visible horizontal plate segmentation` to prevent reads of gilt-metallic armor (this is soft scale, not gold plate armor).
- **`green eyes`** — saturated bright green is the dragon's identity color; avoid `glowing green eyes` (over-reads to magical-eye VFX). Just `bright saturated green` is enough.
- **`tail`** — always describe as `long flexible tail with a leaf-spade tip` to prevent thin rat-tail or club-tail reads.
