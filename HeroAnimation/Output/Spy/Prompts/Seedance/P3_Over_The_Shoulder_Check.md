# P3 — Over-the-Shoulder Check (Seedance 2.0)

> Power movement | 7s | Static locked-off I2V | Source image = `Source/Heroes Stylized/Spy.png`

## Notes for this concept

- The whole loop is a personality beat — no shooting, no VFX beyond the brief speed line on the head snap.
- Head turn back to camera should be slower than the snap away, so the wink lands with weight.
- The nose-tap gesture is non-standard for I2V — may need iteration if the model can't resolve the finger-to-nose contact. If it fails, substitute a simple index-finger-to-lips shh gesture.

Subject:     hooded rogue spy character holding a hand crossbow

Action:      suddenly tensing, snaps his head sharply over his left shoulder
             with a brief speed line, eyes dart paranoidly side to side
             scanning behind him while the crossbow stays still in his
             low-ready grip, pauses for a beat, relaxes his shoulders and
             slowly turns his head back to face the camera, gives a single
             slow wink with his right eye and taps the tip of his pointed
             nose with one finger in a conspiratorial shh gesture, then
             settles back into two-handed low-ready aim. Expression arc:
             sneaking grin -> alert paranoid -> scanning -> relieved wink ->
             sneaking grin. Hood whips with the head snap and settles, quiver
             bolts jostle.

Camera:      50mm, medium-wide, locked tripod, static, full body centered, eye level

@Refs:       img1 = character appearance and canonical starting/ending pose

Style:       Supercell mobile game hero animation, stylized 3D chibi proportions,
             hand-painted saturated colors, bold outlines, Kingshot visual language

Sound:       sharp fabric rustle on the head snap, soft foot scuff on weight shift, faint finger tap on nose during the shh gesture, no voice no dialogue no music

Constraints: pure green chroma key 0x00FF00 background only, static camera no movement or zoom, no blue rim light no environment, seamless loop first and last frame match canonical pose, 7s
