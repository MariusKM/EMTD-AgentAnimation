## Notes for this concept

- **Overall idle direction**: Spy is restless, wired, slightly unhinged in a funny way — this is the "ooh, prey!" outward-focused idle.
- Classic comedic **double-take** timing: snap away → snap back to center → snap away *harder*. The second look is what sells the joke — it's bigger, more eager, more predatory than the first.
- The crossbow lifting a couple of inches on the second look reinforces the "about to strike" read without committing to a full aim.
- The small deflating shrug at the end ("not now") is the comedic release — it's what makes this a loop-safe idle instead of a power move.
- Adds an outward-focused idle to the set — I1 is paranoid-behind, I2 is inward-scheming, I3/I4 are self-referential gear checks, this one is about something *out there*.
