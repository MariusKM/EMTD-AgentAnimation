## Notes for this concept

- **Signature character gag** — gluttonous princess is so spoiled she has an endless supply of cupcakes in her gown pocket. Comedic aggression with the spoon-bat on her own food, then casual "of course I have another one" refill. Lean-hard-into-comedy register.
- **Cupcake flight must exit the top of frame** — do NOT describe it as "flies toward the camera" (Seedance zoom misread). "Flies upward and forward out of the top of the frame" is the safe phrasing.
- **Contact is a LIGHT POP, not a smash** — if the language reads as destructive, Seedance may visually deform or splatter the cupcake mid-frame. Describe the bowl making a "light popping contact" and the cupcake being "sent flying intact."
- **Refilled cupcake must visually match the original** — identical yellow cupcake, icing peak up, same size. Describe explicitly.
- **Pocket placement**: hidden pocket at her right hip (character-anatomical right, which is the cupcake-hand side) in the pink gown. Seedance invents the pocket since it's not explicit in the source art — keep the motion plausible (hand dips down, reaches in briefly, comes up with the replacement).
- **Mouth-closed FFLF** — first and last frames are the closed-mouth smirk with intact cupcake.
- **If the hat-reach variant is preferred later**, rework as "reaches up and tucks her hand into the top of her pink conical hat and pulls out a fresh cupcake" — but Version C (pocket) is the current lock per user direction.
