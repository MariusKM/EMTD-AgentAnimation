## Notes for this concept

- **Concept reframed v2 2026-04-28** — first-pass output (v1) had the diagram bloom outward from a midair anchor point with concentric arcs, which read as too circular and too passive. **v2 reframe**: the architect actively **scribbles/draws the diagram in midair using his calipers as a stylus**, building it up element by element across the frame. Content shifts from concentric arcs to **non-circular technical content with equations** (rectangular schematics, vector arrows, dimension lines, mathematical formulas with Greek letters and numbers, coordinate grids, geometric constructions of triangles and squares). End beat is a **eureka moment** — closed-mouth grin widens, brow shoots up, eyes widen, calipers held high triumphantly. Then the diagram **scatters into particles and disperses** rather than fading inward.
- **No-force theatrical archetype, but now active** — the character does the work in v2 (scribbling) rather than commanding it from afar. Eureka moment is the personality peak.
- **Closed-mouth FFLF** — the eureka moment is a **closed-mouth widened smirk** with widened eyes and raised brow, NOT an open-mouthed gasp. FFLF is preserved.
- **Off-screen mechanism** — equation/diagram lines extend past frame edges off-screen as he scribbles outward. Pin explicitly.
- **VFX intricacy at frame scale** with non-circular emphasis. Named elements per the v2 reframe: rectangular schematic boxes, vector arrows with magnitude labels, fine dimension lines with drafting-style numerical notations, mathematical formulas with Greek letters and numbers, coordinate axes, small geometric constructions of triangles and squares, rich ornamental technical drafting details. Comparison anchor: `like a detailed ornate technical engineering drawing with rich mathematical and ornamental complexity inscribed in midair`. Constraints negate the simple-shape and circular fallback.
- **Discrete-beat language** — anticipation, drawing/scribbling, eureka, dispersal, settle are 5 discrete events. Timecoded markers.
- **Broad-face presentation** of the blade throughout (silhouette-morph rule from P4) — the calipers act as a stylus but the broad face stays presented to camera as he writes.
- **No nose-breath items in Sound** (learned 2026-04-28 across Architect set — see CLAUDE.md). Nose-inhale, nose-exhale, nose-snort items render as snivelling/sniffing audio. Replace with cloth/material/ambient sounds.
- **Safe vocabulary** — `elaborate`, `ornate`, `detailed`, `rich ornamental detail`, `technical engineering drawing`, `mathematical formulas`, `equations`, `scatters`, `disperses`. NO `compass-and-dividers`, `construction circles/triangle`, `vesica-piscis`, `network`, `draftsman's working diagram`, `constructs itself`, `dissolves`, `fragments`.
