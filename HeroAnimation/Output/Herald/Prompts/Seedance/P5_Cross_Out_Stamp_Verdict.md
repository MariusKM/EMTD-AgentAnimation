## Notes for this concept
- **Diagonal cut axis** (axis 4) — must use straight-line strokes rather than arcs. Each diagonal stroke is described as a straight diagonal line in the prompt to prevent Seedance from collapsing them into a circular flourish.
- **Multi-beat enumeration required** — first diagonal, second diagonal, vertical stamp, each gets its own discrete timecoded segment.
- **Negated violence trap** — avoid `cross-out`, `slash`, `strike` as Action verbs even though they describe the action. Use `diagonal stroke`, `quill stroke`, `marks an X-shape`, `stamps the parchment vertically`. Frame as marking, not striking.
- **Disapproving smirk is mid-loop only** — FFLF is still the warm closed-mouth smirk-grin. The disapproving line is a brief tightening; the satisfied open-mouth grin lands only on the stamp finish.
- **`stamp`/`stamping` is fine** — bureaucratic vocabulary, not violence.
- **`unfurl` not `unroll`**.
- **Loop integrity** — the X-mark and stamp ink fully dissipate before loop end (the parchment is unmarked at canonical FFLF).
