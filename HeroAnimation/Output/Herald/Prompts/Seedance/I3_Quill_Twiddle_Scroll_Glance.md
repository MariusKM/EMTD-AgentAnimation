## Notes for this concept
- **Critical `twirl`/`spin` trap** — same risk as Princess Sweet P5 / Fat Princess P4. Must NOT use `twirl` or `spin` anywhere. Use phrasing like `the quill stem rolls between his thumb and index finger`, `the quill head pivots a quarter-turn in his fingers`, `he tilts the quill stem slightly between his fingers`.
- **Object-only rotation** — the quill stem pivots in his fingers; his body and the rest of his arm stay still. Constraints clause makes this explicit.
- **Eyes drift down to glance at the scroll** for ~0.5s, then look up. No head turn — eye motion only with a tiny chin shift.
- **Always-hold both props** — quill stays in left hand throughout, scroll stays in right hand.
- **No `roll`/`rolls` confusion** — using `rolls` for the finger-rotation specifically is OK because it is the exact word for stem-between-fingers rotation, but pair it with `between his thumb and index finger` so the semantic anchor is finger motion not body motion.
