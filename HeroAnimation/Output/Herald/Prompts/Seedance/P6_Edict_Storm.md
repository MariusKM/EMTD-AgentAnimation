## Notes for this concept
- **VFX-heavy spectacle scope** — multiple simultaneous floating glyph paths, off-frame extension, multiple element types (letterforms, sigils, seal motifs, parchment scraps), held peak that fills the foreground. Higher first-pass risk than P1-P5.
- **Recommend running AFTER P1 lands cleanly** so the keyer + prompt craft are tuned to Herald-specific quirks.
- **Loop integrity is the structural risk** — the scroll's two red wax seals stay intact and on the scroll throughout. Floating wax-seal motifs in the storm are SEPARATE VFX elements, not the actual seals.
- **No `proclaim`/`announce`/`declare`/`edict`/`decree`** as nouns in Action body — verdict-beat audio risk. Concept name is "Edict Storm" for the human reader; prompt body uses `floating black ink letterforms`, `golden ornate sigils`, `parchment scraps`.
- **Audio safety pre-emptive** — large dramatic VFX moment fits A6 family 2 mood-music profile. Drop ALL `chime`/`bell`/`tone`/`melodic`/`harmonic`/`warm`/`serene`/`peaceful`/`calmly`. Explicit ban + mechanical-sound positive.
- **Static-camera lock critical** — off-frame extension on multiple sides is exactly what triggers camera-pull-back misread.
- **Element count drift mitigation** — specify ranges, not exact counts.
- **7s duration** for the spectacle scope (rather than 6s).
