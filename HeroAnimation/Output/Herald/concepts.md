# Animation Concepts — Herald

> Stage 2 working doc. Concepts are model-agnostic — prompt translation happens in Stage 3.

## Character Context
- Source art (FFLF input): `Source/Heroes Stylized/Herald.png` — closed-mouth warm smirk-grin, **canonical resting state**
- Open-mouth peak variant (mid-loop only): `Source/Heroes Stylized/mouth_closed/Herald_open.png` — wide-grin / shouting / theatrical climax expression, **never** the FFLF
- Hero description: `Docs/Heroes/Herald.md`
- Personality keywords: cheerful, theatrical, jerky-energetic, bureaucratic-comedic, eager-to-announce, slightly officious
- Weapon: large blue/dark-grey quill feather pen + tan parchment scroll with two red wax seals — the scroll is the canvas, the quill is the weapon
- Natural one-beat action (per client + Silviu): unfurl a small section of scroll → emphatic stabbing scribble with the quill → flourish the quill upward. **Movement style: fast but stiff, lanky** — jerky bureaucratic energy, not grace.
- Tone direction: jovial / slightly comical (client, 2026-04-16) — Herald is the **bureaucratic comedy** lead of the roster ("if the concept of bureaucracy took human form"). Comedy comes from the **stiff over-formality of small actions** — over-the-top scribbles, exaggerated stamps, jerky robotic posture between beats. Not slapstick.
- Animation focus: **bureaucratic theater** — every gesture is performed like a royal proclamation even when the action is small. Quill-and-scroll mechanics drive every beat.

## Hand convention (anatomical, per Seedance/Fat King 2026-04-16 lesson)
- **Anatomical left hand** (viewer's **right** side of image) = **quill** held aloft
- **Anatomical right hand** (viewer's **left** side of image) = **scroll** with two red wax seals presented at chest height
- Pose is **front-three-quarter** (slight body turn toward viewer's right), so viewer-side and anatomical-side are **inverted** for this hero. Be explicit in prompts: always use anatomical terms and call out the viewer-side mapping in the Notes/Constraints when needed.

## Always-hold prop rules
- **Quill stays in left hand always** (anatomical left). Lead the Constraints line in every prompt with `quill stays in left hand always` (short imperative form, per the Fat King 2026-04-16 lesson).
- **Scroll stays in right hand always** (anatomical right). Add second imperative: `scroll stays in right hand always`.
- Note: the client brief's defeat-animation scroll-drop is **defeat-only**. For the hero-screen power and idle loops, both props are always-hold.

## Off-hand prop integrity (FFLF constraint)
- The scroll's **two red wax seals must remain intact and in the same positions** at t=0 and t=loop-end (the FFLF is the same image).
- Concepts that fully unfurl the scroll, break the seals, peel them off, or visibly fill the parchment with ink that wasn't on it at t=0 are **invalid as written** unless the action fully reverses by loop-end (re-coil / wipe / reset to canonical).
- The **partial unfurl with the bottom edge dropping a few inches** is fine — the seals stay visible and the scroll re-coils to canonical at the end. This is the safe envelope for any "unfurl" beat.

## Chroma constraint (Herald's natural VFX palette is chroma-safe)
- Pipeline keys on a **green chroma background** with green-channel despill.
- Herald's signature VFX consists of: **black ink**, **tan/cream parchment glyphs**, **red wax-seal symbols**, **floating letterforms or quill-script motifs**, possibly a **golden royal-decree shimmer**.
- **No green/yellow-green elements** in this palette → no chroma collision → **standard green-chroma generation, no blue-screen variant required**.
- Constraints line should still include a defensive `no green VFX no yellow-green VFX no lime green elements` for belt-and-suspenders, but Herald is a low-risk hero on chroma.

## Stage 3 prompt-craft warnings (carry over to every Herald prompt)
- **`roll`-family filter trigger** (per Architect P2 lesson, 2026-04-27): Seedance silently rejects prompts with high `roll`/`rolled`/`rolls`/`unrolls`/`re-roll` density even when used for parchment scrolls — not body motion. **Authoring rule for Herald specifically**: every scroll-extension beat must use **`unfurl`/`unfurled`/`uncoil`/`re-coil`/`coiled`** as drop-in synonyms. Never `unrolls the scroll`, always `unfurls the scroll`. Never `the scroll rolls back up`, always `the scroll re-coils` or `the scroll uncoils and re-coils`. This is the single highest-risk vocabulary trap for Herald and applies to every power and idle prompt.
- **`twirl`/`spin` reads as full-body rotation** (per Fat Princess P4 / Princess Sweet P5): For any beat where the quill rotates in his fingers (I3 below), avoid both words. Use `the quill stem rolls between his thumb and index finger` / `the quill pivots a half-turn in his hand`.
- **Negated violence words** (per Architect P2, 2026-04-27): the quill scribble is fundamentally a stabbing/poking/jabbing motion onto paper. Resist the temptation to defensively negate (`not a stab not a strike never torn`). Frame positively: `the quill makes a series of small emphatic dots and dashes on the parchment surface, the parchment stays intact and is not torn`.
- **Re-noun the quill as a "writing instrument" when the scribble is the action** (per Architect P2 caliper-blade lesson): even though the quill is the "weapon" by hero-doc convention, in concepts where the action is fundamentally writing (not throwing/firing), use `quill writing instrument` or just `quill pen` rather than `quill weapon` — the verb cluster (`scribbles`, `writes`, `marks`, `notes`) carries no combat semantic field. Only re-noun back to `quill` (or `quill projectile` / `quill dart`) when the action genuinely is forward-fire (P2 below).
- **Closed-mouth FFLF + open-mouth peak**: write the FFLF expression into prompts as `closed-mouth warm smirk-grin`, then describe the climax beat as `the smirk briefly opens into a wide beaming open-mouth grin` for the peak frame, then `closes back to the smirk-grin` for the settle. Don't let the open grin appear at t=0 or t=loop-end.

## Roster differentiation note
- Herald's natural niche is **bureaucratic theater** — quill-and-scroll mechanical beats with comedy through stiff over-formality. This slot is unoccupied across the locked roster. Architect/Wilhelm/Blacksmith/New King run on body-driven weapon mechanics; Spy on narrative beats; Princess Sweet on theatrical VFX-as-power; Moneybags on showmanship; Fat King on regal weight; Fat Princess on appetite-comedy. Herald sits closest to **Spy** (narrative + comedy beats, light body motion, prop-driven) but with bureaucracy as the comedic engine instead of espionage.
- Cross-hero kinetic-shape audit: P3 (overhead flourish) shares the vertical-up axis with New King P3 (overhead rally) and Wilhelm overhead axe — Herald's overhead is **theatrical no-force announcement** with quill flourish + ink-glyph burst, not a strike. Distinguishable. P2 (quill dart fire) shares forward-thrust axis with Architect P4 (broad-face press) and Princess Sweet P9 (forward bloom) — Herald's is a **single clean projectile fire-and-return**, much smaller scope.

## Power Movement Concepts

> Five concepts proposed across distinct kinetic axes. ⭐ = recommended primary.

### P1 — Decree Scribble & Stamp ⭐ *(canonical client-direction beat — bureaucratic comedy, multi-beat sequence)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical hold (~0.3s), his **anatomical right hand flicks the scroll downward** with a sharp jerky motion — the scroll partially **unfurls** a few inches at the bottom, the two red wax seals stay visible at the top of the parchment. He simultaneously brings the **anatomical left hand (quill)** forward and down across his body to meet the scroll surface at chest height. He performs **3-4 emphatic stabbing scribbles** with the quill across the parchment — short fast jerky strokes, each one accompanied by a tiny black-ink puff and a small floating letterform or glyph that briefly hovers above the scroll then drifts away. As the final scribble lands, the **smirk-grin briefly opens into a wide beaming open-mouth grin** — the bureaucratic climax — with a small "got it!" head-bob. Quill flourishes upward to its triumphant canonical position. Scroll **re-coils** back to its canonical partial-roll. Smirk closes back to canonical, settles.
- **Character delivery**: This IS the client-brief description, executed with maximum bureaucratic-comedy commitment. The scribble itself is small in scope but performed with proclamation-level theatricality. The open-mouth grin at the climax sells the joy he takes in completing paperwork. Reads as: "I, royal herald, have officially noted this. It is now decreed."
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.3s), scroll-flick unfurl + quill-arc-down (~0.5s), 3-4 emphatic scribbles (~1.2s — fast jerky stabs with letter-glyph pops), peak open-mouth grin + head-bob (~0.4s), quill flourish up + scroll re-coil (~0.6s), settle (~3s). ~6s total.
- **VFX**: **Tiny black-ink puffs** (3-4) on the scribble beats, each spawning a **small floating letterform or quill-script glyph** in black or sepia ink (~5-7 glyphs total) that briefly hover above the scroll then drift upward and fade within ~0.3s. **Faint golden royal-decree shimmer** along the edges of the unfurled scroll section at the climax beat. No music/orchestral.
- **Secondary motion**: Hat draped tail-tassel sways with the body jerks; feather catches a small flick on the scribble bursts; cloak collar barely shifts (cloak is short, doesn't flow); medallion catches light on the climax grin.
- **Reference**: Spy (narrative beats / acting — small body motion with comedic timing) — but bureaucratic instead of conspiratorial. The kinetic shape is **multi-beat sequence with discrete punctuation** (axis 5) — must be enumerated as discrete timecoded beats in Stage 3 to prevent Seedance from collapsing the scribbles into one continuous arc.

### P2 — Quill Dart Fire *(forward thrust — addresses Silviu's "quill as homing projectile" gameplay note)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (~0.3s), brief **anticipation pull-back** — quill draws back over his anatomical-left shoulder like a small dart, scroll lifts slightly forward in the right hand as a "guide" (~0.5s). Then he **flicks his left wrist sharply forward** and the **quill darts forward away from his hand toward the viewer** with a trailing **black ink streak** behind it — the quill leaves his hand briefly (mid-air projectile beat, ~0.5s). It travels a short distance forward into the foreground, **briefly punctuating an unseen point at arm's length** with a tiny ink-spark, then **arcs back into his left hand on a returning trajectory** (homing-projectile read — the quill always returns to him). Held canonical pose with the quill caught back in hand and a satisfied **smirk-grin briefly opens into a wider grin** ("good shot"). Settles.
- **Character delivery**: The "quill as weapon" reading from Silviu's notes — quill is a thrown homing projectile in gameplay, this is the hero-screen version. Body motion stays composed (he's a herald, not a warrior); the quill does the aggressive work via VFX. Closest he gets to a "combat" beat without breaking the bureaucratic-theater character.
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.3s), pull-back anticipation (~0.5s), forward flick + quill projectile + ink streak (~0.5s), brief punctuation at arm's length + ink-spark (~0.3s), homing return arc into hand (~0.5s), satisfied wider-grin held catch (~0.4s), settle (~3.5s). ~6s total.
- **VFX**: **Black ink streak/comet trail** behind the quill on both the outbound flick and the return arc. **Small black ink-spark** at the punctuation point in mid-air. **Faint golden royal-stamp shimmer** on the spark moment. Strictly no green elements (chroma-safe).
- **Secondary motion**: Hat tail-tassel snaps on the forward flick and settles on the catch; feather flicks; medallion catches light on the satisfied grin; cloak edge shifts on the wrist motion.
- **Reference**: Architect P4 (forward press / riposte) for the structural anchor — but quill-as-projectile-VFX driven instead of blade-driven, comedic instead of fencer-precise.
- **⚠ Generation-risk notes**:
  - **Static-camera prefix critical** — projectile leaves the frame and returns. Lead Subject with `static camera shot of...` per Spy P1 lesson. Add explicit Constraint `the camera does not pan or follow the quill the camera stays locked the quill is the only thing moving forward toward the viewer`.
  - **Re-noun cluster**: in this concept the quill IS the weapon, so `quill projectile` / `quill dart` / `the quill flies forward` is fine. Avoid `stab` / `pierce` / `strike` for the punctuation beat — use `marks` / `punctuates` / `flicks against` instead.
  - **Quill-leaves-hand vs. always-hold rule**: this concept is the **single exception** to `quill stays in left hand always` — the quill briefly leaves the left hand (~0.5-1s mid-shot) and returns. Constraints line for THIS concept only: `quill leaves the left hand briefly during the dart-and-return beat, the quill returns to the left hand before the loop end and stays in the left hand for the final settle, the quill is never dropped or held in the right hand`. Loop integrity: quill is in left hand at t=0 AND t=loop-end.

### P3 — Royal Proclamation Flourish *(vertical-up + theatrical no-force — the ceremonial "hear ye" beat)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (~0.4s), **anatomical left arm raises the quill straight overhead** in a slow theatrical arc, while simultaneously the **anatomical right hand thrusts the sealed scroll forward and slightly upward toward the viewer** at chest height — the **"hear ye, hear ye!" announcement pose**. Held theatrical peak (~1s) with both arms extended, the **smirk-grin briefly opens into a wide beaming open-mouth grin** as if mid-shout, head tilted slightly back. At the peak, a **floating cluster of golden ink-glyphs and small floating letterforms** burst outward from above the scroll in a fan shape — like a royal proclamation made visible. The glyphs are mostly black-ink letterforms with a few golden royal-decree symbols (a small crown sigil, a quill-and-scroll motif, a flourish curl). The glyphs hold for a beat then drift outward and fade. Quill flourishes back down to its canonical aloft position, scroll returns to chest height, smirk closes back to canonical, settles.
- **Character delivery**: The most overtly theatrical of the slate — the comedy is in the **scale of the announcement gesture** vs. how small the actual content is (he's just performing his job). The open-mouth grin at the peak makes the "I am announcing something glorious" read explicit. Bureaucratic ceremony at full theatrical volume.
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.4s), simultaneous quill-up + scroll-forward to peak (~0.7s), held theatrical peak with open-mouth grin + glyph burst (~1.2s), glyphs fan outward and start fading (~0.6s), arms return + smirk closes (~0.6s), settle (~2.5s). ~6s total.
- **VFX**: **Black-ink letterform glyph cluster** (~6-8 small floating letters and quill-script symbols) bursting upward and outward from above the scroll in a fan shape. **2-3 golden royal-decree symbols** mixed in (small crown sigil, quill-and-scroll motif, flourish curl) for the "official proclamation" read. Glyphs drift outward and fade within ~0.6s after the peak. **Faint golden shimmer** along the edge of the scroll throughout the peak. No music/orchestral.
- **Secondary motion**: Hat tail-tassel lifts on the overhead arm raise; feather catches light at the peak; medallion gleams; cloak collar barely shifts; both feet stay planted.
- **Reference**: New King P3 (overhead rally with peak VFX) for the vertical-up structural anchor. Differentiated by: (a) Herald has both hands extended (rally vs. announcement), (b) VFX is letterform glyph cluster vs. light/sword shimmer, (c) tone is bureaucratic-ceremonial-comedy not solemn-resolute.
- **⚠ Generation-risk notes**:
  - **No quoted speech** in Action body (per Architect P10 v4 lesson, 2026-04-28) — the temptation here is huge ("hear ye, hear ye!" is the literal direction). Express the announcement beat through expression and pose only: `the smirk briefly opens into a wide beaming open-mouth grin as if mid-announcement, head tilts slightly back`. Do NOT write "as if shouting hear ye" or include any quoted text. Sound field uses standard `no voice no dialogue no speech no vocalizations no humming no music` extended ban (verdict-beat scene per Architect lesson).
  - **No `proclaim`/`proclamation`/`announce`/`announcement`/`declare`** as nouns in the Action body — these may cue audio model toward voiceover. Use `the herald lifts both arms in a ceremonial pose` / `the herald presents the scroll forward in a ceremonial gesture`. The concept name calls it Proclamation for the human reader; the prompt body avoids the noun.
  - **Audio safety risk** — large dramatic VFX moment (overhead flourish + glyph burst) may fit A6 family 2 mood-music profile. Apply preemptively: drop ALL `warm`/`serene`/`peaceful` Action modifiers; explicit Constraints ban on music/orchestral/swells; mechanical-sound-only positive constraint (parchment crinkle on the scroll-forward, fabric shift of the cloak, faint brass-medallion tick at peak).

### P4 — Scroll Over-Unfurl Slapstick *(multi-beat sequence with comedy — bureaucratic mishap)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (~0.3s), the **anatomical right hand flicks the scroll downward** to unfurl a small section — but the flick is too forceful and the scroll **unfurls way more than intended**, the bottom edge dropping rapidly past his hip and then nearly to his boots in a comedic over-extension. He **freezes mid-jerky-motion** with a startled wide-eyed look (the open-mouth peak variant — eyes slightly wider than normal, mouth in a small "oh!" shape, but using the same mouth-open expression as the peak variant). Recovery beat: he gives the scroll a sharp jerky **upward yank** (anatomical right wrist snap), and the scroll **rapidly re-coils** back to its canonical partial-roll with a small parchment-crinkle. Still slightly flustered, he composes himself with a small **bureaucratic shoulder-roll** and the smirk-grin reasserts itself. Final beat: he gives the **quill a small triumphant flourish** (small wrist motion, anatomical left hand) as if to say "everything is under control." Settles to canonical.
- **Character delivery**: Pure bureaucratic-mishap comedy — the joke is his momentary loss of dignified composure and quick recovery. He's the herald, he doesn't make mistakes — but he just did, and he's pretending he didn't. The shoulder-roll-and-flourish recovery is the comedic punctuation. This is the **most overtly Supercell-jovial** of the slate.
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.3s), too-forceful scroll-flick + over-unfurl drop (~0.7s), startled freeze with mouth-open "oh!" beat (~0.6s), sharp wrist-snap re-coil (~0.5s), bureaucratic shoulder-roll recovery (~0.7s), small triumphant quill flourish + smirk reassertion (~0.5s), settle (~2.7s). ~6s total.
- **VFX**: **Small black-ink puff** at the moment the scroll over-unfurls (single emphatic puff). **Tiny parchment-dust motes** around the scroll on the rapid re-coil. **Small golden composure shimmer** along the medallion + belt at the recovery flourish (the "I meant to do that" sparkle). All chroma-safe. No music/orchestral.
- **Secondary motion**: Hat tail-tassel **bounces hard** on the over-unfurl freeze (the hat reacts to his startled body); feather flicks dramatically; cloak shifts on the shoulder-roll recovery; belt medallions catch light on the final flourish; one foot may shift weight slightly during recovery (subtle).
- **Reference**: Spy (narrative beats with comedic timing — narrative arc within a single power loop) + Moneybags (theatrical recovery / showmanship). The kinetic shape is **multi-beat sequence with discrete punctuation** (axis 5) — must enumerate beats discretely in Stage 3.
- **⚠ Generation-risk notes**:
  - **Loop integrity is the structural risk** — the scroll must be at the canonical partial-roll position (with seals visible) at t=0 AND t=loop-end. The over-unfurl drop is mid-loop only; the re-coil must complete cleanly. Constraints line: `scroll is in canonical partial-roll position with both red wax seals visible at the start and at the end of the loop, the over-unfurl drop and re-coil happen in the middle of the loop only`.
  - **Off-frame risk** — the over-unfurled scroll bottom edge may drop near or past the bottom frame edge. Lead Subject with `static camera shot of...` and add explicit Constraint that camera stays locked. Describe the over-unfurl extent as `the bottom edge of the scroll drops rapidly to about knee level` (specific anchor) rather than open-ended language that might cue Seedance to follow with a tilt-down.
  - **`unfurl` not `unroll`** — applies twice in this concept (initial unfurl + over-unfurl extension). Never write `unrolls` or `over-unrolls`. Always `unfurls` / `the scroll unfurls way more than intended` / `the scroll over-extends`.
  - **Open-mouth "oh!" beat** — this concept uses the open-mouth variant for a startled "oh!" expression rather than a wide grin. Stage 3 prompt should describe the peak as `the smirk briefly opens into a small surprised round mouth-open expression with eyes slightly wider, then closes back to the smirk-grin on the recovery`. The FFLF is still the closed-mouth canonical.

### P5 — Cross-Out & Stamp Verdict *(diagonal cut + decisive vertical stamp — bureaucratic disapproval)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (~0.3s), he gives the scroll a sharp small unfurl flick to expose a section. Then he performs a **single decisive diagonal stroke** with the quill — **upper-left to lower-right** across the scroll surface (a clear "X" half — diagonal cut axis), with a sharp jerky bureaucratic motion. Brief held pose mid-stroke (~0.2s). Then a **second diagonal stroke** in the opposite direction (upper-right to lower-left) — completing the X-mark **cross-out**. Held canonical-stiff "verdict" pose for ~0.6s with the closed-mouth smirk-grin tightening into a small disapproving line and the brow knitting slightly (judgmental bureaucrat). Then the final beat: **the quill comes down vertically with a sharp stamping motion** onto the scroll — a decisive **stamp-finish** with a small black-ink starburst. Brief peak smirk widens to the open-mouth grin (the satisfaction of having delivered an official verdict). Quill flourishes back up to canonical, scroll re-coils, settles.
- **Character delivery**: The bureaucrat-with-authority beat — Herald isn't always cheerful, sometimes he's the one delivering bad news with an X-mark. The sequence reads as a clear narrative: assess → cross out → stamp verdict. The closed-mouth disapproving line during the verdict hold + the open-mouth satisfied grin on the stamp finish is the **comedy whiplash** that lands the bureaucratic personality.
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.3s), scroll unfurl flick (~0.3s), first diagonal stroke + held mid-stroke (~0.6s), second diagonal stroke completing X (~0.4s), held verdict pose with disapproving smirk (~0.6s), vertical stamp + ink starburst + open-mouth satisfaction grin (~0.5s), quill flourish up + scroll re-coil + smirk reassert (~0.7s), settle (~2.6s). ~6s total.
- **VFX**: **Black-ink trails** along each diagonal stroke (visible ink streak following the quill nib). **Small black-ink starburst** at the vertical stamp moment with **3-4 tiny floating "rejected" or X-mark glyphs** that briefly drift upward then fade. **Faint red-wax shimmer** around the scroll seals at the verdict moment (subtle — the seals themselves don't move/break). No music/orchestral.
- **Secondary motion**: Hat tail-tassel jerks with each stroke (3 sharp jerks — left-to-right, right-to-left, down); feather flicks on each beat; cloak collar barely shifts; medallion catches light on the verdict satisfaction grin.
- **Reference**: Architect P4 (broad-face press — single decisive forward beat) — but Herald's version is multi-beat with the X-then-stamp sequence. **Diagonal cut/sweep axis** (axis 4) — must use **straight-line strokes** rather than arcs in the prompt, and explicitly describe each stroke as a **straight diagonal line** to prevent Seedance from collapsing them into a circular flourish (per Architect Stage 5 review lesson).
- **⚠ Generation-risk notes**:
  - **Negated violence trap**: avoid `cross-out`, `slash`, `strike` as Action verbs even though they describe the action. Use `diagonal stroke`, `quill stroke`, `marks an X-shape`, `stamps the parchment vertically`. Frame the action positively as writing/marking, not striking.
  - **Multi-beat enumeration required** (per Architect P4 lesson) — must give each diagonal stroke and the stamp their own discrete timecoded segment in the Action body. Run-on description will collapse to one continuous arc.
  - **`stamp`/`stamping` is fine** — these are bureaucratic vocabulary, not violence. The "stamp finish" reads as official-document stamping (royal seal, approval/rejection mark).
  - **Disapproving smirk is mid-loop only** — the FFLF is still the warm closed-mouth smirk-grin, not the disapproving line. Stage 3 prompt: `the warm smirk-grin briefly tightens into a small disapproving line during the verdict hold beat, then opens into a wider satisfied grin on the stamp finish, then closes back to the warm smirk-grin for the settle`.

### P6 — Edict Storm *(VFX-heavy spectacle — slate-mandatory big-scope concept)*
- **Origin**: Slate-mandatory VFX-heavy concept (per pipeline rule — every hero gets one big-scope power concept regardless of personality fit). For Herald, the spectacle reading is **the royal proclamation made fully manifest** — every edict he has ever delivered erupting outward at once.
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (~0.4s), brief **anticipation** — quill draws back over his anatomical-left shoulder, scroll pulls slightly toward his chest in his right hand (~0.5s). Then he **simultaneously thrusts the scroll forward at chest height with his right hand AND sweeps the quill in a sharp horizontal arc across in front of him with his left hand** — the two-handed "decree it forth" gesture. As the quill arc completes, **a massive eruption of floating black ink letterforms, golden royal sigils, parchment scraps, quill-script glyphs, and red wax-seal symbols bursts outward from the scroll and the path of the quill** in a swirling storm. The storm spreads outward in a wide fan, several elements **streaming off the left and right edges of the frame**, others arcing upward past the top of the frame and downward past his hips. The storm holds at peak for ~1s — Herald stands at the center with the scroll still presented forward, quill held aloft on the follow-through, **smirk briefly opens into the wide beaming open-mouth grin** at the spectacle climax (the herald's ultimate joy: a fully-witnessed proclamation). Then the storm **collapses inward and dissipates** — letterforms drift back toward the scroll, golden sigils fade, parchment scraps disintegrate into faint dust motes that fall and fade. Quill returns to canonical aloft, scroll re-coils to canonical partial-roll, smirk closes back, settles.
- **Character delivery**: The "this herald is actually a hero-tier proclamation-mage" beat. Body motion stays composed-bureaucratic (small two-handed gesture, not big athletic motion) — the **VFX storm IS the power**. Reads as: "behold, the full weight of the royal record." Closest in feel to Princess Sweet P6 (Thorn Forest Bloom) in scope and risk profile, but the spectacle vocabulary is paperwork-magic instead of plant-magic.
- **Timing feel**: Canonical hold (~0.4s), anticipation pull-back (~0.5s), simultaneous scroll-thrust + quill-sweep (~0.5s), explosive storm eruption outward across the frame (~0.8s), held storm peak with open-mouth grin climax (~1s), collapse-inward retract + fade (~1.2s), settle (~1.6s). ~6s total (may extend to 7s for full spectacle scope).
- **VFX**: **Massive black-ink letterform cluster** (~15-25 floating letters and quill-script glyphs) extending outward across the frame in a wide swirling fan, several streaming past the left and right edges and the top of the frame. **5-8 small golden royal sigils** scattered throughout (small crown sigils, quill-and-scroll motifs, flourish curls) — the "official decree" elements. **3-5 small red wax-seal symbol motifs** floating in the storm (NOT the scroll's actual seals — those stay intact on the scroll). **Tan/cream parchment scraps** drifting like leaves through the storm. **Soft golden royal-decree shimmer** filling the foreground as the underlying glow. On collapse: letterforms and sigils drift back toward the scroll then fade, parchment scraps disintegrate into faint cream dust motes that fall and fade within ~1.2s. Strictly no green elements (chroma-safe — Herald's whole VFX palette is naturally chroma-safe). No music/orchestral.
- **Secondary motion**: Hat tail-tassel lifts hard on the storm eruption and settles on the collapse; feather catches dramatic light at peak; cloak collar shifts on the body weight transfer; medallion gleams at the climax grin; both feet stay planted (composed bureaucratic stance throughout).
- **Reference**: Princess Sweet P6 (Thorn Forest Bloom) for the spectacle structural template — explosive outward growth + held peak + retract-and-fade, with off-frame elements. Herald's version replaces the living thorn-forest with a paperwork-storm: same scope, different vocabulary.
- **⚠ Generation-risk notes**:
  - **Higher I2V difficulty than P1-P5.** Multiple simultaneous floating glyph paths, off-frame extension, multiple sigil/seal/scrap element types, and a clean retract that returns to FFLF is significantly more complex than a single-flick or single-stroke beat.
