# Animation Concepts — General

> Source: `Source/Heroes Stylized/General.png`
> Hero doc: `Docs/Heroes/General.md`
> Personality: Disciplined, stoic, dutiful, stern, vigilant, professional
> Weapon: Short straight sword — held point-down in two-handed sentinel grip on the pommel; right hand on top (dominant)
> Tone: Serious-leaning per user direction (2026-05-04). Comedy beat is minimal — a dry stoic smirk, not theatrical comedy.

## Vocal Direction
General is a stoic military professional. Vocal personality is **dry, restrained, low-volume**: a single quiet stoic exhale through the nose on a strike, a measured wordless grunt of effort on a heavy lift, no laughs, no chuckles, no theatrics. On idles, soft natural calm ambient nose-breathing only — no audible vocalizations beyond the breath.

## Pose / Hand Convention
Two-handed sentinel grip with both hands on the pommel of the sword, sword held point-down centered in front of the body. **Anatomical right hand on top** (dominant) and left hand below. For one-hand power-move beats (raise to salute, shoulder-load for slash), the **right hand** is the active sword-arm; the left releases to the side or stays braced low at the hilt.

## Power Movement Concepts

> Seven concepts spanning six kinetic axes — no-force theatrical pose, forward thrust, horizontal arc, vertical down (twice — P4 motion-led, P6 VFX-led), diagonal cut, multi-beat with off-screen elements. Cross-roster audit: differentiated from New King (one-handed regal sword) by leaning hard into two-handed military discipline; differentiated from Wilhelm (heavy axe slam) by mid-weight contained motion with no foreshortened-toward-camera follow-through; differentiated from Diana (agile flourish) by single-strike discipline, no double-tap, no flourish.

### P1 — Sentinel's Salute
- **Weapon action**: From the sentinel two-handed grip, the right hand lifts the sword off the pommel and raises it vertically in front of the face/helmet, blade pointing straight up — chivalric blade-up salute. Held briefly. Lowers blade back to the pommel grip.
- **Character delivery**: Heels click together coming to attention. Brow furrows slightly, jaw set, stoic eyes forward. Single sharp inhale through the nose; chest fills under the breastplate. Tiny dry smirk on the lower beat.
- **Timing feel**: Crisp, precise, military. Snap up (~0.4s), held salute (~1.5s), measured lower (~0.6s).
- **VFX**: Tight clean blade gleam — single specular highlight that travels up the blade as it raises.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape lifts subtly off the back from the heel-click, settles. Red plume nods once with the head dip.
- **Kinetic axis**: No-force theatrical pose.
- **Reference**: Static-anchored ceremonial beat — closest to no existing reference. Carries the client brief headline.

### P2 — Disciplined Forward Thrust
- **Weapon action**: Right hand snaps up off the pommel, both hands re-grip the hilt with the right hand on the grip and left bracing low; he steps a short half-step forward and drives a single straight two-handed thrust at chest height; pulls back into sentinel guard.
- **Character delivery**: No flourish, no chamber wind-up — military efficiency. Eyes lock on the target line. Mouth a hard horizontal line. Single dry stoic head-dip on the recover.
- **Timing feel**: Slower and weightier than Diana — short tight anticipation (~0.4s), explosive thrust (~0.3s), held extended (~0.8s), measured pull-back (~0.7s). Heavier than Diana's flick, lighter than Wilhelm's slam.
- **VFX**: Sharp clean white **straight-line blade trail** along the thrust path — tight forward streak, not an arc. Tighter and more disciplined than Diana's wide swoosh.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape billows once on the step-forward, snaps back on the recover. Plume kicks once on the thrust apex.
- **Kinetic axis**: Forward thrust.
- **Reference**: Diana but heavier and disciplined — single thrust, no double-tap.

### P3 — Crisp Horizontal Cut
- **Weapon action**: Right hand pulls the sword off the pommel into a one-handed shoulder-load on the anatomical right; one decisive horizontal slash sweeps across the body from anatomical right to left at chest height; left hand catches the hilt at the recovery and sword returns to two-handed sentinel guard.
- **Character delivery**: Short shoulder-coil before the strike, weight shifts onto the back foot, then explosive uncoil. Dry stoic exhale through the nose on the cut. Eyes track the blade line.
- **Timing feel**: Mid-weight — anticipation (~0.4s), explosive horizontal arc (~0.25s), trail dwell (~0.6s), settle (~0.7s).
- **VFX**: Single tight horizontal **white blade trail** — sharper, tighter, and more disciplined than Diana's sweeping arc; no second slash, no flourish.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape flares horizontally during the swing, snaps back. Beard catches the breath of the swing.
- **Kinetic axis**: Horizontal arc / sweep.
- **Reference**: Diana's first slash — single, disciplined, heavier, no fancy follow-through.

### P4 — Sword Flick + Stoic Point *(replaced 2026-05-04 — Two-Handed Overhead Cleave dropped, did not generate well; kinetic axis was redundant with P6)*
- **Weapon action**: From sentinel, right hand alone pulls the sword off the pommel and gives a quick contained wrist flick above the right shoulder (small tight arc, like an old veteran's habit of settling the blade), then smoothly extends the sword forward one-handed at chest height with the blade tip pointing straight forward at the imagined enemy and viewer; left hand drops to brace at the hip during the flick and the held point. Held in the stoic intimidating point pose for the middle of the loop with eyes locked forward in a stare-down, then back to two-handed sentinel.
- **Character delivery**: Pure stoic intimidation — no smirk, no theatrical flourish. The wrist flick is a small contained gesture (veteran's habit, not showy). The held point is the personality anchor: brow knit hard, eyes lock forward through the blade line, jaw set.
- **Timing feel**: Quick contained flick (~0.4s), smooth extend forward (~0.5s), held intimidating point (~2.5s — the personality anchor), measured return (~0.7s).
- **VFX**: None — no blade trail. Polished sword catches a single clean specular highlight along the blade during the held point. Distinct from P2 (which has a white forward-trail) by being one-handed, slower, and held.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts on the flick, settles outward during the held point. Plume nods once on the flick. Beard stays steady. The stillness of the held point is the read.
- **Kinetic axis**: Forward extend with held point (one-handed) — distinct from P2 (two-handed thrust with trail) by being one-handed, slower, and held rather than struck.
- **Reference**: Closest to no existing reference — a held-pose stare-down anchored in a forward-axis silhouette.
- **Stage 3 prompt risks**: Static-camera prefix mandatory (forward-extend toward viewer = camera-zoom misread risk). The flick is a contained wrist motion only — the body does NOT lean forward, and the blade extends only to chest height in front of him not toward the camera lens. No trail VFX (clean differentiator from P2).

### P6 — Sword-Plant Lightburst *(vertical down — VFX-heavy)*
- **Weapon action**: From sentinel grip, both hands raise the sword straight overhead (slow, weighty); he drives the blade **point-first downward into the ground** in front of him, the pommel ending up at chest height with both hands locked on top — a decisive standard-planted finishing move. Holds the planted pose, then draws the blade smoothly back up to sentinel guard.
- **Character delivery**: Stoic and serious throughout — no smirk, no theatrical flourish. Eyes lock down on the strike point; brow knit hard. Single quiet stoic exhale through the nose on the plant. He **stays planted** for the VFX peak — the stillness sells the weight.
- **Timing feel**: Slow disciplined raise (~0.8s, showing weapon weight), explosive plant (~0.25s), held planted with VFX peak (~1.5s), measured pull-up to sentinel (~0.7s).
- **VFX (the headline)**: Layered white-gold energy effect at the moment of plant —
  - **Multi-layer blade trail** down the strike path (3–4 stacked white-gold streaks) that lingers ~0.5s
  - **Outward expanding shockwave ring** of white-gold light radiating across the ground from the strike point — clean concentric ring, ~1m diameter
  - **Radial light spokes** projecting outward from the strike point along the ground (4–6 short straight beams, like a compass-rose burst)
  - **Bright impact flash** at the contact point (single frame burst, then decays)
  - **Dust ring puff** at the ground (physical, kicked up by the impact)
  - **Sparks** flicking up from where steel meets ground
- **Secondary motion**: Cape billows up violently on the raise, snaps fully outward on the plant, settles slowly during the held VFX peak. Plume kicks hard on the plant. Beard catches the breath of the swing. Cape is doing heavy lifting on this concept.
- **Kinetic axis**: Vertical down (shared axis with P4, but VFX-led rather than motion-led — and ends point-down rather than chest-height stop, so the silhouette differs).
- **Reference feel**: Closest to Wilhelm's overhead slam for weight + cape physics, but the VFX (not the foreshortening or follow-through) carries the impact. Stoic delivery keeps it military rather than theatrical.
- **Stage 3 prompt risks**: VFX intricacy upgrade pattern from CLAUDE.md — describe the lightburst as `an elaborate ornate radial burst with multiple fine concentric shockwave rings, fine straight light spokes radiating outward at regular intervals, layered blade trail streaks, and a bright impact flash` joined as connected prose. Filter-safe verbs — `plants the blade point-down into the ground`, `the blade meets the ground`. Avoid `stab, strike, pierces` even in negation.

### P5 — Diagonal Guard Cut
- **Weapon action**: Right hand pulls the sword off the pommel into a high-right shoulder-load (blade up on the anatomical right); single sharp diagonal cut from upper-right down to lower-left across the body; left hand re-catches the hilt at the bottom and sword returns to sentinel guard.
- **Character delivery**: Short coil into the shoulder-load, weight on the back foot, explosive diagonal release. Stoic eyes locked on the cut line. Brow knit hard.
- **Timing feel**: Mid-weight — anticipation (~0.4s), diagonal explosive (~0.25s), trail dwell (~0.5s), recover (~0.7s).
- **VFX**: Tight **straight-line diagonal blade trail** from upper-right to lower-left — straight-line, not arced; distinct from Diana's wide arc and Wilhelm's vertical slam.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape billows diagonally on the cut, snaps back. Plume nods on the release.
- **Kinetic axis**: Diagonal cut / sweep.
- **Reference**: New shape not covered in the reference set — closest to Diana's slash energy in a non-horizontal axis.

### P7 — Volley Command *(multi-beat — VFX-heavy, signature concept)*
- **Weapon action (multi-beat, timecoded)**:
  - **(0.0–1.0s)** From sentinel, the right hand lifts the sword off the pommel; both hands raise the sword straight overhead — signal-raise, brow knit, eyes locked forward
  - **(1.0–1.6s)** Held overhead — the order is being given
  - **(1.6–2.0s)** Sword chops sharply forward and down to chest height — the **fire-signal cut**
  - **(2.0–3.8s)** A tight **volley of arrows streams from behind-and-above him**, arcs cleanly over his head and through the upper frame, exiting forward-up out of frame at the top. Arrows have white feathered fletchings, dark steel arrowheads, polished brown shafts, and short white motion-blur trails.
  - **(3.8–4.5s)** General watches the volley arc away — stoic forward gaze, jaw set, no expression change. Sword starts settling back toward sentinel.
  - **(4.5–6.0s)** Sword returns to two-handed sentinel grip; canonical pose.
- **Character delivery**: Pure stoic command — no smirk, no head-shake, no shrug. He gives the order with **eyes and the sword**, not with his face. The stillness during the volley is the personality beat: he ordered the fire and now stands as the immovable line behind it.
- **Timing feel**: Slow disciplined raise → held signal → sharp chop → sustained volley arc → measured settle. The contrast between his stillness and the volley's speed is the whole point.
- **VFX (layered, headline)**:
  - **The volley itself** — eight to twelve fletched arrows in a tight parallel formation, each with a short white motion-blur trail. Volley arcs cleanly from behind-and-above him, **passes over his head above the cape silhouette**, exits out the top of frame forward-up. Foreshortening on the lead arrows as they near the upper edge gives the "toward viewer" reading without a literal zoom.
  - **Sword down-cut blade trail** on the fire-signal chop — tight straight white trail along the chop line
  - **Wind-rush wake** across cape and plume on the moment the volley passes overhead — the air the arrows displace catches his cape edges and lifts the plume
  - **Subtle dust motes** kicked up from the ground at his feet on the sword chop (he plants weight, not blade)
- **Secondary motion**: Cape billows up on the sword-raise, snaps down on the chop, catches the **wind-wake** on the volley pass-over, settles slowly. Plume kicks once on the chop and twice on the volley wind-wake. Beard catches the breath of the chop.
- **Kinetic axis**: Multi-beat sequence with off-screen elements. The only concept in his slate using this axis — high production value, highest generation risk.
- **Reference feel**: No reference covers this — it's the most cinematic concept in the General slate, and the strongest "leader of armies" beat the hero screen can carry.
- **Stage 3 prompt risks**:
  - **Static camera prefix mandatory** — lead Subject with `static camera shot of...`; never let Seedance read "into camera" as a zoom
  - **Arrow placement language** — `arrows stream from behind and above him, arc cleanly over his head above the cape silhouette, and pass through the upper frame exiting forward and upward out of view at the top of the frame` — keeps arrows above the cape (no morph) and out of his back-shoulder zone
  - **Concrete arrow description** (intricacy upgrade) — `eight to twelve fletched arrows in a tight parallel volley formation, each with white feathered fletchings, dark steel arrowheads, polished brown shafts, and short white motion-blur trails behind each shaft`
  - **Filter-safe action verbs** — `arrows stream, arc, pass, fly` — avoid `pierce, strike, hit, impact target, kill`
  - **No body lean** — he stays upright; "leans forward" reads as zoom (Spy lesson)
  - **Vocal direction** — single quiet stoic exhale on the chop, nothing else; no battle cry, no command shout. Sound carries the drama: synchronized bowstring snaps off-screen behind him, fletching whistle, doppler-passing whoosh as the volley clears overhead, sharp sword-cut whoosh on the chop.
  - **Discrete timecoded beats in the Action body** mandatory — multi-beat without timecodes will collapse into one continuous motion (Architect Stage 5 lesson)
  - **Duration**: 6s minimum (the multi-beat structure needs the room — 5s would compress the volley pass-over)

## Idle Movement Concepts

### I1 — Breathing baseline *(mandatory)*
- **Action**: Continuous ambient breathing only — slow calm rhythm of small chest rises and falls under the breastplate, no discrete visible breath events.
- **Expression**: Held resting stoic — slight frown, jaw set, eyes forward. No expression arc.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts almost imperceptibly with the ambient breathing; one slow blink during the loop.
- **Duration feel**: 4s steady loop.

### I2 — Vigilant Watch *(optional expressive)*
- **Subtle action**: Eyes track slowly from far-left to center to far-right (subtle, no head-turn — eye-only) across the loop, as if scanning the field. Hands stay locked on the pommel.
- **Expression**: Disciplined watch — slight squint mid-scan, returns to neutral at center.
- **Breathing/weight**: Calm ambient breathing throughout. No weight shift.
- **Secondary motion**: Plume shifts a few millimeters with the implied head micro-pivot (kept eye-led).
- **Duration feel**: 4s loop.

### I3 — Pommel Re-Settle *(optional expressive)*
- **Subtle action**: Small disciplined re-grip on the pommel — the right hand lifts barely off the pommel, settles back into a slightly tighter grip; left hand follows microseconds later. Reads as a soldier checking his weapon stays steady.
- **Expression**: Eyes flick down to the pommel for an instant, return forward. Small dry stoic exhale.
- **Breathing/weight**: Ambient breathing, micro weight shift onto the back foot.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape catches the small body shift with a tiny drift. Plume nods microscopically with the head dip.
- **Duration feel**: 4s loop.
