## Notes for this concept

- **VFX-heavy concept — should be SPECTACULAR** (revised 2026-05-05 per user feedback — v2/v3 outputs read too toned/restrained). The full VFX stack on the plant: (1) blade glows brilliant white-gold during the raise and peaks at the apex; (2) layered white-gold blade trail streaks down the strike path on the downward swing; (3) a tall brilliant **pillar of white-gold light shoots straight upward** from the contact point past the top of the frame at the moment of impact (the new headline element); (4) elaborate ornate radial burst with concentric shockwave rings outward across the ground; (5) fine straight light spokes radiating outward in a compass-rose burst; (6) bright clean impact flash; (7) physical dust ring puff and a strong wave of sparks; (8) the planted blade itself glows brilliant white-gold along its length during the held peak.
- **Anticipation and weight on the windup** (added 2026-05-05) — the slow lift is HEAVY and DELIBERATE with visible weight. Shoulders coil back as the sword rises, chest fills under the breastplate, jaw clenches harder. Brief held-apex beat with the blade glowing brilliantly and the body coiled before the explosive drop. This is Wilhelm-style weight anticipation applied to General.
- **VFX intricacy upgrade pattern** (Architect P1/P5 lesson) — describe the lightburst concretely with elaborate ornate language and rich ornamental detail joined as connected prose. Comparison anchors: `like an elaborate ornate ribbon of light cascading down behind the blade`, `like flowing ribbons of white-gold light spiraling up the pillar`. Without that, Seedance collapses to a single thin generic glow ring.
- **Color is white-gold not green** — green VFX would be eaten by the chroma key. White-gold reads as righteous knight energy and survives keying.
- **Filter-safe verbs** — `plants the blade point-down into the ground`, `the blade meets the ground`. Avoid `stab`, `strike`, `pierces` even in negation per the Architect P2 lesson.
- **Character delivery is stoic and still** — no smirk, no theatrical pose. He stands as the immovable line behind the lightburst.
- **One-handed raise, two-handed plant, two-handed return** (revised 2026-05-05 per user feedback on v2 — the v1 ending had a redundant one-handed pull-up + left-hand-returns sequence that read as a pointless "lift hand and place it back where it already was"). The right hand alone lifts the sword off the pommel and raises it overhead while the left hand drops to the side at attention; on the way down to plant, the left hand comes back up to join the right hand on the pommel for the planted two-handed pose. **Return is now both hands together** — the held planted pose carries through to almost the end of the loop, and the blade just smoothly rises a small amount out of the ground in the final fraction of the loop with both hands staying locked together on the pommel. No separate hand-lift, no hand-release, no two-step grip change. The held planted pose IS already very close to the canonical sentinel pose (same hands on pommel, blade just in the ground rather than hovering), so the return is a single smooth lift not a multi-step transition.
- **Static-camera prefix mandatory** — vertical motion + ground impact is a known camera-tilt misread case.
