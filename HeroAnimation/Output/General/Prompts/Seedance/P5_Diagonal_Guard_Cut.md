## Notes for this concept

- **Diagonal axis from upper-anatomical-right to lower-anatomical-left** — distinct from P4 (vertical) and P3 (horizontal). Straight-line trail, NOT an arc.
- **One-handed shoulder-load on anatomical right** — the right hand pulls the sword up high to a shoulder-load position with the blade angled high on his anatomical right side, the left hand drops to brace at the hip during the load and the cut, then catches the hilt at the bottom and the sword returns to two-handed sentinel.
- **Static-camera prefix mandatory** — diagonal slashes are exactly the kind of fast diagonal motion Seedance can read as a tilted pan.
- **Single sharp diagonal cut** — no double-tap, no flourish. The straight-line trail is the discipline marker.
- **Trail is straight-line not arced** — explicit in Constraints. Differentiates from P3 horizontal arc and Diana wide swoosh.
- **Filter-safe verbs** — `cuts`, `sweeps diagonally`, `comes down across`. Avoid `slashes through`, `cleaves apart`. The cut ends in empty air below his anatomical-left side.
