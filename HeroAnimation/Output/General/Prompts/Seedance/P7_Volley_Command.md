## Notes for this concept

- **One-handed signal-raise and chop** — right hand only lifts and chops the sword; left hand drops to his side until the very end of the loop when it returns to the pommel. Two-handed grip only at the first and last frame.
- **Volley releases on the chop, not after** — the bowstring snap and the chop apex are the same moment; arrows are already streaming over his head as the chop reaches chest height.
- **Arrows fly point-first** — sharp metal tip leading, feathers trailing at the back. Plain language; avoid technical archery terms.
- **No sword trail VFX** — the sword cuts cleanly. The arrows are the only headline VFX.
- **Static camera, no body lean** — Seedance can read forward action as a camera push-in. Locked.
- **Arrows originate behind him in depth, not from the side** (revised 2026-05-04 — earlier generations had arrows curving in from screen-right). Anchor the origin as "the vanishing point directly behind his back in the deep background, small in the distance" and the flight path as "straight forward toward the camera, growing larger with depth, passing over his head." Negate side-entry explicitly.
