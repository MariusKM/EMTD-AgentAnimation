## Notes for this concept

- **Replaced concept** — original P4 (Two-Handed Overhead Cleave) did not generate well; kinetic axis was redundant with P6. New P4 is a one-handed sword flick + held stoic forward point. Personality through stillness rather than motion.
- **One-handed throughout** — right hand alone lifts the sword off the pommel, performs a small contained wrist flick above the right shoulder, extends the sword forward to chest height with the right arm extended; left hand drops to brace at the hip during the flick and the held point; left hand returns to the pommel only at the very end of the loop as the canonical sentinel grip is restored.
- **Wrist flick is small and contained** — old veteran's habit of settling the blade. Tight arc above the right shoulder, NOT a swing or a slash. No blade trail.
- **The held point is the personality anchor** — ~2.5 seconds of held stoic intimidating stare-down with the blade extended forward. Seedance is good at held poses; this concept plays to that strength.
- **Static-camera prefix mandatory** — forward-extend toward viewer is a known camera-push-in misread case. Locked up front and reinforced in Constraints.
- **No body lean** — the body stays upright at canonical depth. Only the right arm extends. "Leans forward" reads as zoom (Spy lesson).
- **No blade trail VFX** — clean differentiator from P2 (two-handed thrust with white forward trail). Only a clean specular highlight along the blade during the held point.
- **Filter-safe verbs** — `extends`, `presents`, `points forward`. Avoid `thrusts`, `stabs`, `pierces`, `aims at`. The held point is presented to empty air in front of him.
- **Vocal direction** — single quiet stoic exhale equivalent expressed as plate-armor settle + cape cloth shift on the held point. NO nose-breath items per CLAUDE.md.
