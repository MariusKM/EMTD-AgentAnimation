## Notes for this concept

- **Closest to a combat read on the slate** — vines extend forward as if wrapping an invisible target, then withdraw. Body motion stays light and graceful; the vines do the aggressive work.
- **Vocabulary discipline** (per content-flag A3 + A4) — original concept word "lash" was potentially combat-flagging. Action body uses safer language: vines `extend forward`, `coil around an unseen point`, `briefly hold`, `recede`, `draw back into the stem` — no `lash`, `whip`, `strike`, `bind`, `snap`. The concept name stays "Forward Vine Lash" for human readability but the prompt body avoids the trigger vocabulary.
- **Chroma rule** as P1 — olive yellow-green vines, no bright/lime/spring green.
- **Tomboy reveal beat** as P1 — brief mischievous half-grin on the recede ("yes, I just did that").
- **Static-camera prefix** — vines extending forward toward the viewer is exactly the kind of forward-axis motion Seedance reads as a camera push-in. Lock down up front.
- **Always-hold rose** at start of Constraints. Right-hand wrist absorbs a small recoil on the recede.
