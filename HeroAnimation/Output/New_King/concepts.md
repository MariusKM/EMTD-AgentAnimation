# Animation Concepts: New King

> Source art: `Source/Heroes Stylized/New_King.png`
> Hero description: `Docs/Heroes/New_King.md`
> Personality keywords: Gentle, kind, strong, confident, experienced, heroic, approachable, warm, leader
> Weapon/signature item: Silver sword with gold-and-emerald crossguard
> Canonical pose: Three-quarter view, sword held diagonally across body at chest height with both hands on grip, weight slightly forward, maroon cape draping behind

---

## Power Movement Concepts (standalone 6s loop)

### P1: "The Royal Salute"
- **Action**: From his two-handed chest grip, the King lifts the sword upward in a smooth arc — blade rising to point skyward in a one-handed salute at face level. Holds the salute. Then brings it back down in a controlled arc, both hands returning to the diagonal chest grip. Settles back to the canonical pose.
- **Emotional arc**: Steady confidence → proud resolve at salute peak → warm gentle smile → calm return
- **Timing** (6s): 1s lift → 1.5s salute hold → 1.5s controlled return → 2s settling/breathing back to pose
- **VFX**: Golden gleam traveling along the blade as it rises, emerald gems flash at the peak
- **Secondary motion**: Cape lifts during upward motion, settles on return. Crown catches light. Scroll pouch sways.
- **Internal reference**: Wilhelm's authority without the aggression

### P2: "The Confident Slash"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, quick pull-back to the right, then a wide horizontal slash from right to left. Blade continues through a full arc, looping back to the original diagonal grip. Character settles back into the canonical pose.
- **Emotional arc**: Focused determination → fierce intensity at the slash → satisfied confidence → calm return
- **Timing** (6s): 1s wind-up → 1s slash with trail → 1.5s deceleration and return to grip → 2.5s settling/breathing
- **VFX**: Warm white-gold slash trail following the blade arc, fading over ~1.5s
- **Secondary motion**: Cape swirls outward during slash, wraps and settles on return. Belt pouch swings. Hair shifts.
- **Internal reference**: Diana (slash trail, speed) + Wilhelm (composed return)

### P3: "The Defender's Rally"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, releases left hand and thrusts the sword one-handed high into the sky — arm fully extended overhead, blade straight up, head tilts back to follow the tip of the blade upward, eyes locked on the tip. Brief commanding pause at the peak as a VFX-style star gleam flares off the blade tip. Then a decisive diagonal chop — the blade sweeps down through a wide arc in front of the body, decelerating at chest height. Left hand returns to the grip, settles back into the canonical two-handed diagonal pose with the gaze forward again.
- **Emotional arc**: Determined resolve → commanding rally at peak (head up, eyes on blade) → decisive follow-through on the chop → steady warmth → gentle return
- **Timing** (6s): 1s lift (arm + head rise together) → 1s hold at peak (with tip gleam) → 1.5s chop down in arc, decelerate at chest → 0.5s left hand re-grips → 2s settling/breathing
- **VFX**: Bright 4-point star gleam bursts off the blade tip at the overhead peak, then fades before the chop begins. Subtle gold motion trail following the blade through the down-arc.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape billows dramatically up and back during the lift, trails the blade on the chop, settles on return. Scroll pouch swings. Crown tips with the head movement.
- **Internal reference**: Wilhelm's overhead rally structure, but one-handed, lighter, and warmer — the upward gaze on the blade reads as "look what I wield" rather than aggression.

### P4: "The Guardian's Flourish"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, releases left hand and rotates the sword in a smooth one-handed vertical flourish (tight circle, blade flashing). A second, wider flourish. Left hand returns to grip, back to canonical pose.
- **Emotional arc**: Calm confidence → playful skill during flourish → warm knowing smile → composed return
- **Timing** (6s): 0.5s release → 1.5s first flourish → 1.5s second wider flourish → 0.5s return to grip → 2s settling/breathing
- **VFX**: Blade catches warm key light during rotation, brief circular gleam trails
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts gently from body shifts. Crown catches light.
- **Internal reference**: Spy's personality-driven pacing — character-revealing action

### P6: "The Commander's Point"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, releases left hand and extends the sword one-handed directly toward the viewer — arm nearly fully extended, blade perfectly horizontal, tip aimed forward at camera. Holds the point with a fierce determined gaze and a subtle forward lean. Pulls back smoothly, left hand returns to grip, settles back into the canonical pose.
- **Emotional arc**: Focused resolve → commanding authority at the point → steady confidence → calm return
- **Timing** (6s): 1s extend → 1.5s point hold → 1.5s retract and re-grip → 2s settling/breathing
- **VFX**: Golden shimmer travels along the blade as it extends; at the point peak, a **bright VFX-style star gleam** (4-point sparkle) bursts off the blade tip, then fades before the retract. Emerald gems on crossguard flash at the peak.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape swings back slightly as the torso leans into the point, settles on return. Scroll pouch sways. Crown catches light.
- **Internal reference**: Wilhelm's commanding presence translated into a forward-projecting beat (vs. P1's upward salute). Plays directly to the viewer — strongest "hero screen" read.

### P7: "The Sovereign's Oath"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, raises the sword smoothly to vertical in front of the face — blade straight up, both hands on the hilt — and briefly touches the flat of the crossguard to the forehead in an oath-on-the-blade gesture. Holds the oath with closed eyes for a beat, then opens eyes with renewed resolve, lowers the blade in a controlled arc, and settles back to the canonical pose.
- **Emotional arc**: Quiet reverence → solemn resolve at the oath → renewed determination → composed return
- **Timing** (7s): 1.5s raise → 2s oath hold (forehead touch + eye close + reopen) → 1.5s lower → 2s settling/breathing
- **VFX**: A soft warm-gold inner glow builds on the blade during the oath, peaking as eyes reopen; emerald gems on the crossguard pulse gently in sync with the exhale.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape settles close to the body during the oath (stillness), subtle drift on the lower. Breath visible as slow chest rise/fall during the hold.
- **Internal reference**: Medieval knighting iconography — slower, more emotional beat than the others. Shows the "gentle, experienced leader" side. Contrasts with P2/P6's energy.

### P8: "The Builder's Decree"
- **Action**: From the two-handed chest grip, the King lowers the sword tip-down and **plants the blade firmly into the ground in front of him** — the sword standing upright like a marker, both hands releasing the hilt. Both hands then draw a rolled parchment from the scroll pouch, raise it to chest height, and unfurl it fully with a decisive two-handed gesture as if issuing a decree. Eyes flick down to read, then back up to the horizon with a small confident nod. Scroll is rolled back and returned to the pouch, both hands grip the planted sword, pull it smoothly from the ground, and raise it back into the canonical diagonal chest grip.
- **Emotional arc**: Measured focus → decisive authority at the unfurl → approving confidence → composed return
- **Timing** (8s): 1s plant sword (tip to ground, release) → 1s draw scroll with both hands → 2s unfurl and decree beat → 1s stow scroll → 1.5s re-grip, pull sword from ground, raise to canonical → 1.5s settling
- **VFX**: Subtle dust puff where the blade tips into the ground; warm light catches the parchment as it unfurls; a brief soft gold shimmer on the scroll's edge at the decree peak. No blade VFX — the scroll is the focus.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape shifts forward slightly as he leans to plant, settles around the planted sword during the decree. Scroll pouch flexes as parchment is drawn/replaced. Crown catches light on the head lift. Blade stays anchored upright through the middle of the beat — a strong vertical silhouette element.
- **Internal reference**: Leans hard into the **Builder identity** (military *and* administrative leader) — the sword *grounds* the beat literally and visually, while the scroll is the focus. Pairs thematically with I4 "Builder's Glance" as a power-beat version of the same signal. The planted-sword silhouette is iconic knight/ruler imagery.

## Idle Movement Concepts (standalone loop)

### I1: "The Watchful King" (4s)
- **Subtle action**: From canonical pose, gentle weight shift and a slow head turn to the side — surveying his kingdom — then turns back. Hands stay on sword grip throughout.
- **Expression**: Warm, contented half-smile
- **Breathing/weight**: Steady chest rise and fall. Grounded.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts in ambient breeze, scroll pouch sways slightly, crown catches light shifts

### I2: "The Steady Breath" (4s)
- **Subtle action**: Stays in canonical pose — gentle breathing motion (chest rise/fall), very slight rhythmic grip adjustment on sword. Minimal head movement, perhaps a slow blink.
- **Expression**: Quiet confidence, gentle smile, eyes forward
- **Breathing/weight**: Steady deep breathing, weight centered and stable
- **Secondary motion**: Cape gentle drift, blade catches light subtly, belt accessories shift with breathing

### I3: "The Gentle Nod" (4s)
- **Subtle action**: From canonical pose, a slow nod — as if acknowledging a subject — combined with a breath that puffs the chest slightly. Returns to rest.
- **Expression**: Kind, approachable smile
- **Breathing/weight**: Breath expanding chest, then settles
- **Secondary motion**: Cape sway, crown shifts minutely with the nod, emerald gems catch light

### I4: "The Builder's Glance" (4s)
- **Subtle action**: From canonical pose, right hand releases the sword grip and dips briefly to the scroll pouch at his hip — fingertips brush or half-draw a rolled parchment. Eyes flick down to it for a beat, then back up to the horizon. Hand returns to the sword grip, canonical pose restored.
- **Expression**: Thoughtful, considered — a small approving nod as he looks back up. Warm, not grim.
- **Breathing/weight**: Weight shifts subtly to the hip side as the hand dips; recenters on return. Steady breath throughout.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts from the small weight shift, scroll pouch moves as the hand engages it, crown catches light on the head lift
- **Internal reference**: Nods to the "Builder" identity — military *and* administrative leader. The scroll pouch is already canonical in the source art, so I2V has a concrete anchor.

## Revised Concepts (2026-04-22 — client weapon-driven rule)

> Appended after client feedback (2026-04-22): power move = weapon action, not ability or personality-only beat. Every hero gets a mandatory breathing-only baseline idle. See `HeroAnimation/Docs/StyleGuide_Animation.md` §2 and `HeroAnimation/PromptTemplates/animation_concepts.md` Core Rules.
>
> **Audit of existing New King power concepts against the new rule**:
> - ✅ P1 Royal Salute — passes (sword lifted into salute)
> - ✅ P2 Confident Slash — passes (sword slash with trail VFX)
> - ✅ P3 Defender's Rally — passes (overhead sword rally + chop)
> - ✅ P4 Guardian's Flourish — passes (sword flourish)
> - ✅ P6 Commander's Point — passes (sword extended forward)
> - ✅ P7 Sovereign's Oath — passes (sword oath gesture)
> - ❌ P8 Builder's Decree — explicitly "no blade VFX — scroll is the focus." The weapon is planted (grounded, not used) while the scroll carries the beat. Fails the weapon-driven rule. Revised into P9 below.
>
> **Idle audit**: I2 Steady Breath is closest to the mandatory baseline but still includes a "slight rhythmic grip adjustment." I0 below is the cleanest fit.

### P9 — Builder's Decree, Sword Primary *(weapon-primary revision of P8)*
- **Weapon action**: From canonical (two-handed diagonal sword grip at chest), he raises the sword with both hands into a **decisive forward salute at chest height** — blade horizontal, extended forward toward the viewer, both hands on grip. Holds the decree pose with a commanding leader's gaze. Then, without lowering the sword, his left hand releases and briefly **gestures outward to the side** in a "hear ye, my subjects" addressing motion while the right hand holds the sword steady. Left returns to the grip, sword rotates back into the canonical diagonal chest grip, settles.
- **Character delivery**: Warm leader making a decree — the sword anchors his authority; the addressing gesture is layered on top as a personality beat rather than stealing focus. Warm confident smile throughout, small approving nod at the end.
- **Timing feel**: 1s lift to extended decree, 1s held decree (with gesture), 1.5s return to grip, 2.5s settling breathing.
- **VFX**: Warm gold gleam traveling along the blade on the lift into decree, emerald gems on crossguard pulse softly at the decree peak. No dust/plant effect (the sword is not grounded anywhere in this beat).
- **Secondary motion**: Cape swings forward slightly on the lift and settles, scroll pouch sways with the weight shift, crown catches light on the head lift. Blade is the visual anchor throughout.
- **Reference**: General sword salute structure + warmer New King register. Realizes the "Builder" identity via the decree gesture layered on the weapon — not at the weapon's expense.
- **Duration**: 6s
- **Why revised**: Original P8 planted the sword in the ground to free both hands for the scroll — the weapon was de-emphasized to a vertical silhouette element. The revised P9 keeps the sword primary (held, lifted, extended) and reduces the scroll/decree signal to a simple free-hand addressing gesture. The "Builder" identity still reads; the weapon stays central.

### I0 — Breathing Baseline *(mandatory)*
- **Subtle action**: Canonical pose preserved exactly — two-handed diagonal sword grip at chest height, weight forward, maroon cape draping behind. Slow deep chest rise and fall. Tiny weight shift between breaths. Nothing else — no head turn, no nod, no grip adjustment, no hand dip to pouch.
- **Expression**: Held warm confident smile. Occasional slow blink.
- **Breathing/weight**: Deep steady chest breathing, stance rock-solid, both hands unchanged on the sword grip.
- **Secondary motion**: Cape drifts very minimally with breath, crown absolutely still, belt pouch still.
- **Duration**: 4s
- **Why added**: Satisfies the client mandatory-baseline idle rule. I2 Steady Breath was close but included a "slight rhythmic grip adjustment" and a blink — I0 is stricter (blink only, no grip movement). I1-I4 remain as expressive variants on top of this baseline.
