## Notes

- The sword is **planted tip-down into the ground** at the start — blade standing vertical and stable, hilt up, both hands free. Small dust puff on impact.
- Planted sword remains upright and still through the scroll beat — it reads as a **vertical silhouette anchor**, iconic knight/ruler imagery
- Scroll is drawn, unfurled, and rolled back **with both hands** — no one-handed scroll handling
- At the decree peak (scroll fully open), a brief soft gold shimmer catches the parchment's edge; eyes read down then lift with a small confident nod
- No blade VFX on this one — the scroll is the focus, the sword grounds the beat
- Cape shifts forward slightly as he leans to plant the sword, settles around the planted blade during the decree, drifts naturally on the return
- Scroll pouch flexes as the parchment is drawn and replaced; crown catches light on the head lift
- Expression arc: measured focus → decisive authority at the unfurl → approving confidence → composed return
- Timing: 1s plant sword → 1s draw scroll → 2s unfurl and decree beat → 1s stow scroll → 1.5s re-grip and pull sword from ground → 1.5s settling back to canonical pose
- Start and end frame: canonical two-handed diagonal chest grip (source pose) — loop must close cleanly
