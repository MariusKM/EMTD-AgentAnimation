Action:      releases left hand from the sword grip and thrusts the sword one-handed high into the sky — right arm fully extended straight overhead, blade perfectly vertical and pointing directly upward — head tilts back to follow the blade, eyes locked on the tip, holds a brief commanding rally pause at the peak as a bright VFX-style star gleam sparks off the blade tip and casts a brief warm flash on the crown and upturned face, then **snaps the sword down in a fast powerful chop** through a wide diagonal arc in front of the body — the blade whips through the air with a strong gold motion trail streaking behind it, torso driving into the swing — hard decisive stop at chest height with a crisp impact freeze, left hand whips back onto the grip, head lowers and gaze returns forward, settles back into the original two-handed diagonal chest grip

## Notes

- Blade must be **perfectly straight and vertical** at the overhead peak — arm fully extended, crossguard horizontal, tip pointing directly at the top of frame
- **One-handed lift with right hand only** — left hand releases at the start of the motion; this is NOT a two-handed overhead raise
- Head and eyes track the blade tip upward during the lift, stay locked on the tip through the peak pause, return forward smoothly during the chop
- At the peak, a **bright 4-point VFX star gleam** flares off the blade tip (anime-style shine), then fades before the chop begins. The gleam itself briefly lights the crown and the upturned face — this is the only new light in the scene.
- Chop-down is **fast, powerful, and decisive** — the blade whips through a wide diagonal arc in front of the body (not a straight vertical drop) with clear speed lines / motion blur on the blade and a strong gold motion trail. The swing lasts well under a second. Ends in a **hard, crisp impact freeze** at chest height — momentum visibly stops, not a soft deceleration. Contrast with the slower reverent lift sells the power beat.
- Torso and shoulders drive into the chop — it's a full-body swing, not a limp-wristed forearm flick. Weight shifts forward on the impact.
- Cape billows dramatically up and back during the upward lift, then snaps violently downward/outward trailing the blade through the chop, finally settles on the return. Crown tips with the head movement. Scroll pouch swings.
- Expression arc: determined resolve → commanding rally at peak (head up, eyes on blade) → fierce focus on the chop → steady warmth → gentle return with a small confident smile
- Timing: 1s lift (arm and head rise together) → 1.25s peak hold with tip gleam → 0.75s fast powerful chop with hard impact stop → 0.5s left hand re-grip → 2.5s settling / breathing back to canonical pose
- Start and end frame: canonical two-handed diagonal chest grip (source pose) — loop must close cleanly
